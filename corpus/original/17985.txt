Choe Seung-chol, a senior North Korean Workers' Party official who led policy toward South Korea during the South's previous administration, was reportedly executed for making wrong judgements on Seoul, according to sources. A South Korean Unification Ministry spokesman said Choe's execution had not yet been confirmed.
North Korea's trade with China increased to 73 percent of its total trade with 91 countries excluding South Korea in 2008 from 32.7 percent in 2003, implying its economic dependency on China has worsened. South Korean state-run trade and investment promotion agency KOTRA said on Monday that North Korea's total exports for 2008 climbed 23 percent to $1.13 billion from the previous year and imports rose 32.7 percent to $2.69 billion.
Seoul National University Hospital announced Monday that it would accept and honor written "Do Not Resuscitate" (DNR) statements from patients suffering from terminal cancer, to support a patient's right to halt "meaningless and excessive treatment." ($1=1260.2 Won)
