US copper ends almost 2 pct down as commods plummet
2009-07-29 16:48:00.0
(Reuters) - Copper settled down almost 2 percent in New York on Wednesday as commodities were hit by a stronger dollar, weak stock markets, a heavy build in U.S. oil inventories and drop in orders for durable U.S.-made goods.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for September delivery HGU9 ended down 4.30 cents, or 1.7 percent, at $2.4775 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX's final estimated futures volume at 24,556 lots versus Tuesday's 23,141m lots.
* "There's concern that the market's got ahead of fundamentals for the time being. I wouldn't be surprised to see the September contract fall back to around 2.25 to where we are right now. It looks toppy. I think the path of least resistance is on the downside right now." - Matthew Zeman, head of trading at LaSalle Futures Group in Chicago.
* U.S. stocks fell as investors worried that China's banks might be poised to hit the brakes on lending to stem market excesses, a move that could curb the global economic recovery. [.N]
* Copper for three-months delivery on the London Metal Exchange settled down $115 at $5,415 a tonne.
* Stocks of copper in LME warehouses fell 575 tonnes to total 278,350 tonnes. However, cancelled warrants -- material tagged for delivery out of warehouses -- fell by 1,825 tonnes, meaning more material is available.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU down 393 short tons to 55,799 short tons as of Tuesday.
