-- Rescuers took advantage of the clear weather early on Thursday to pick up 1,241 survivors in helicopters in southern Taiwan, where Typhoon Morakot triggered a series of mudslides and flattened villages.
-- Nearly 400 villagers could have been buried alive in Hsiao Lin, a small village in Taiwan's southern county of Kaohsiung after a mudslide was triggered by torrential rains from a typhoon, according to disaster officials.
-- More than 34 countries said they would like to offer help to Taiwan, with Singapore saying that it will make a donation of about $200,000 and deliver food and medication to the typhoon-battered island.
-- Citigroup said a possible drop in panel prices could be delayed to November from its previous forecast of September, due to shortages after an earthquake disrupted glass production in Japan.
-- Taiwan's cabinet has decided to set aside a special budget of up to T$200 billion ($6.1 billion) for reconstruction after a typhoon rattled the island.
