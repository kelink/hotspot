The LCD maker said it will sell 4 billion new shares to the two companies at T$2.50 per share, it said in a filing to the Taiwan stock exchange, but did not specify how much Tatung and Compal were buying.
Compal said in a separate statement that it will invest no more than T$7 billion in the share placement, which it was pursuing to improve its supply chain management.
"Given the intensified competition in the high-tech industry, increase in customisation, the supply chain management is more critical now," Compal Chief Financial Officer Gary Lu said in the statement.
The Tatung group is CPT's largest shareholder. Tatung's listed subsidiaries include home appliance maker Tatung Co (
CPT's larger rivals AU Optronics (
) and Chi Mei Optoelectronics 3009.TW have seen a turnaround recently, largely on the back of greater demand from China as a result of its own stimulus package to boost domestic spending.
CPT and Compal made the announcements after the Taiwan stock exchange closed on Thursday. CPT shares were down 1.27 percent, Compal was up 4.41 percent, while the benchmark TAIEX share index
was up 0.62 percent. (Reporting by Kelvin Soh; Editing by Jacqueline Wong)
