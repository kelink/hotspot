A lower U.S. dollar further fueled the rise in ADRs, as investor appetite rises for foreign equities traded in cheaper U.S. currency.
The ICE Futures' dollar index .DXY tumbled 1.25 percent.
The Bank of New York Mellon index of leading European ADRs .BKEUR rose 1.6 percent. In Europe, the FTSEurofirst 300
index of top shares closed at its lowest in nearly six weeks, dragged lower by financials.
Receipts with the Bank of New York Mellon index of leading Latin American ADRs .BKLA jumped 2 percent, while major Latin American markets were mixed.
