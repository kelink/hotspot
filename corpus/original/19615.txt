* Copper for July delivery HGN9 rose 4.40 cents, or 2.2 percent, to $2.0350 per lb by 10:53 a.m. EDT (1453 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX estimated electronic futures volumes at 7,688 lots by 9 a.m.
* COMEX copper up in short-covering bounce on back of firmer open on Wall Street and dollar weakness - Frank Lesh, broker and futures analyst with Future Path Trading in Chicago.
* Copper's upside momentum gathered pace after data showed new orders for big-ticket manufactured goods fell a less-than-expected 0.8 percent in March. [ID:nN23464766]
, adding to copper's morning momentum. [USD/]
* Uncertainties surround market chatter that China's State Reserve Bureau has begun selling some of its recently accumulated copper inventories. [ID:nHKG203306]
* Copper inventories in warehouses monitored by the Shanghai Futures Exchange fell 34 percent to 15,051 tonnes from 22,705 tonnes a week ago.
* London Metal Exchange (LME) copper stocks dropped 10,925 tonnes to 429,550 tonnes on Friday. <LME/STX1>
* Canceled warrants stood at 65,800 tonnes on Thursday -- about 16 percent of total stocks -- from 69,175 tonnes the previous day.
* COMEX copper stocks fell by 106 short tons to 47,565 short tons as of Thursday.
* Japan's output of rolled copper products fell a record 60 percent from a year earlier in March. [ID:nT301196]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 last traded at $4,425 a tonne, up $83 from Thursday's close. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
