NYMEX-Crude bounces as equities up, dollar dips
2009-07-30 11:17:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. crude futures bounced back strongly on Thursday as renewed optimism about the economy lifted equities markets and pulled down the dollar, sidelining Wednesday's worries over rising oil inventories.
"Markets are rebounding with stocks higher, renewed optimism and a weak dollar. Oil markets were oversold after yesterday's sharp drop and jobless data may have also provided additional support," said Tom Bentz, analyst at BNP Paribas Commodity Futures Inc.
The number of U.S. workers filing new claims for jobless benefits rose slightly more than expected last week, but the four-week moving average for new claims fell. [ID:nLU173294]
Earlier, world stocks and commodity prices rose after China's central bank reaffirmed its commitment to "appropriately loose" monetary policy and on European company earnings. [MKTS/GLOB]
The report defied expectations for a draw, as imports jumped and refinery utilization fell. [EIA/S]
* On the New York Mercantile Exchange at 11 a.m EDT (1500 GMT), September crude CLU9 was up $2.88, or 4.55 percent, at $66.23 a barrel, trading from $62.76 to $66.48.
* In London, September Brent crude LCOU9 rose $3.30, or 4.96 percent, to $69.83 a barrel, trading from $66.36 to $70.20.
* NYMEX August RBOB RBQ9 surged 8.30 cents, or 4.47 percent, at $1.9380 a gallon, trading from $1.8450 to $1.9477.
* NYMEX August heating oil HOQ9 leaped 8.22 cents, or 4.92 percent, to $1.7535 a gallon, trading from $1.6601 to $1.7627.
* The spread between the current front month and the five-year forward crude contract CLc61 was at $17.56, based on the September 2014 contract Wednesday settlement at $83.79. The spread ended Wednesday at $20.43.
