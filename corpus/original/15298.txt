Technicals, Wall St push US copper to 2-week highs
2009-06-25 14:20:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures ended higher on Thursday after hitting their highest levels in nearly two weeks, as a firmer tone in equities and positive technical momentum buoyed the market in a run-up to the end of the second quarter.

* Copper for September delivery HGU9 gained 3.50 cents, or 1.5 percent, to settle at $2.3160 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Session range from $2.2450 to $2.3465, its highest level since June 15.
* Market up in short-term technical correction from this week's sell-off below key support at $2.20 a lb - John Gross, publisher of the Copper Journal.
* Technical buy-signal triggered from an overnight hurdle of the Wednesday session peak of $2.3020 - Larry Young, senior trader at Infinity Futures Inc in Chicago.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 27,119 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Wednesday at 24,571 lots.
* Open interest up 708 lots to 110,244 contracts as of June 24.
* Gross domestic product, which measures total output within U.S. borders, dropped at a 5.5 percent annual rate in the first quarter after shrinking 6.3 percent in the fourth quarter of last year and 0.5 percent in the third quarter. [ID:nN25259990]
* Closed arbitrage windows between the Shanghai and London Metal Exchange markets, the summer slowdown and speculation that China's SRB may be looking to sell copper continue to muddy the short-term picture - RBC Capital Markets.
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> dropped 3,450 tonnes to 271,600 tonnes on Thursday to their lowest level since mid-November.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU lost 295 short tons to 59,765 short tons as of Wednesday.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 closed up $75 at $5,130 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; editing by Jim Marshall)
