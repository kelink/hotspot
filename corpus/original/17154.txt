US copper hits 7-month peak on weak dollar, China data
2009-06-01 10:35:00.0
(Reuters) - The price of copper rose to its highest level since mid-October in New York futures trade Monday morning as an extended decline in the dollar and positive manufacturing data from China, the world's top copper consumer, drove the bullish momentum.

* Copper for July delivery HGN9 climbed 9.40 cents, or 4.3 percent, to $2.2915 a lb by 10:24 a.m. EDT (1424 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Morning range from $2.1825 to $2.2945, the contract's loftiest level since October 14.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 10,110 lots by 10 a.m.
* COMEX copper rally rooted in overseas market strength after positive purchasing manager index data from China provides fresh evidence of recovery in the world's third-largest economy.
* China's official purchasing managers' index at 53.1 marked the third consecutive month of expansion of activity in manufacturing. [ID:nPEK17043]
* "The ISM suggests that the U.S. economy is stabilizing, if not outright improving and the market is all about risk appetite coming back. Generally, this is still a case of better-than-expected data not necessarily good for the U.S. dollar. We expect no rebound in sight for the dollar just yet." - Shaun Osborne, chief currency strategist with TD Securities in Toronto.
* Unexpected 0.8 percent uptick in U.S. construction spending in April adds to the industrial metal's bullish tone. [ID:nN01456282]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> went down 300 tonnes to 311,975 tonnes on Monday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- fell to 36,700 tonnes from 38,825 tonnes the previous session.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU eased 18 short tons on Friday, bringing total warehouse levels to 55,664 short tons.
* Inventory levels of key automotive industrial metals aluminum, copper and steel to remain swollen due to ailing auto demand in the U.S. [ID:nL1544368]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 was last traded at $5,011 a tonne, up $181 from Friday's kerb close. It rallied earlier to a 7-month peak at $5,018. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
