US copper ends down in pause from new 9-mth peak
2009-07-21 14:53:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures ended lower on Tuesday, after scaling fresh 9-month highs earlier in the session, as investors booked profits near psychological resistance at $2.50 per lb.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for September delivery HGU9 ended down 1.80 cents at $2.4510 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Range from $2.4305 to $2.4945, a new high dating back to mid-October.
* COMEX estimated final futures volume at 19,694 lots, against Monday total of 21,798 lots.
* Open interest up 2,002 lots to 114,049 contracts open as of July 20.
* Copper ends down in mild profit-taking retreat after recent price strength meets investor upside profit objectives - Ralph Preston, futures analyst with HeritageWestFutures.com in San Diego, California.
* "From a market standpoint, the recent upward movement in copper prices reflect some important fundamentals about this business that are going to be important for the long run, and that is the requirements for copper in China and the developing world" - Chief Executive Officer Richard Adkerson.
* Bernanke, in testimony before the House Financial Services Committee, said unemployment was likely to remain high into 2011, which he warned could undermine consumer confidence and derail a recovery. [ID:nWEQ001226].
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> up 475 tonnes at 266,425 tonnes on Tuesday.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU down 132 short tons at 59,119 short tons as of Monday.
* World refined copper consumption exceeded production by 37,000 tonnes between January and April this year, versus a deficit of 147,000 tonnes in the same year-ago period - the International Copper Study Group (ICSG). [ID:nN20129704]
* China's imports of refined and alloy copper rose to 402,544 tonnes in June, from May's 354,567 tonnes, official customs data showed on Tuesday. [ID:nHKG122823]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 ended up $90 at $5,400 a tonne, after hitting a new 9-month high at $5,469. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Christian Wiessner)
