* Copper for July delivery HGN9 was trading down 0.95 cent at $2.3560 a lb by 9:58 a.m. EDT (1358 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Early range from $2.3460 to $2.3955, a new high for the benchmark July contract dating back to mid-October 2008.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 7,090 lots by 9 a.m.
* Copper eases at the open as investors book profits and prices consolidate after Tuesday's sharp rally - Larry Young, senior trader at Infinity Futures Inc in Chicago.
* Commodity bulls firmly in charge as weak dollar, budding hopes for a global economic recovery and solid chart patterns combine to push prices higher - MF Global analyst Edward Meir.
* Market sentiment lifted by local media reports in China that the country's industrial production rose by 8.9 percent in May from a year earlier. [ID:nPEK63329]
* London Metal Exchange copper warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> dropped 2,075 tonnes to 294,275 tonnes on Wednesday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- continued to decline, falling to 29,075 tonnes on Tuesday and equivalent to 9.9 percent of total inventory levels.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU edged up 27 short tons to 57,513 short tons as of Tuesday.
* Zambia's copper production in the four months to end-April rose to 218,101 tonnes compared with 192,064 tonnes for the same period last year. [ID:nWEA6320]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 rallied to a new eight-month high at $5,264 a tonne. It last traded at $5,163 from $5,170 on Tuesday. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; editing by Jim Marshall)
