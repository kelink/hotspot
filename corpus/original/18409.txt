The company, which cumulatively reduced its total workforce by 47.2 percent during the six months ended March 31, said it had also begun a program to reduce employee salaries by up to 30 percent from the salaries prevailing at the end of the first quarter.
For the first quarter, the company reported a loss of $3.9 million, or 9 cents a share, compared with earnings of $28.4 million, or 63 cents a share, in the year-ago period.
Excluding items, it reported a loss of 2 cents a share.
One analyst expected the company to post a loss of 1 cent a share, before items, on revenue of $110 million, according to Reuters Estimates.
The China-based company said sales in its LCD segment and consumer electronics and communication segment dropped by 25.3 percent and 48.5 percent, respectively, while telecommunication components assembly unit revenue declined 11.1 percent.
Shares of the company closed at $4.30 Monday on the New York Stock Exchange. (Reporting by Bijoy Koyitty in Bangalore; Editing by Himani Sarkar)
