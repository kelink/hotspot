"One of the reasons that equity markets in China have been up so much is from bank lending for projects," said Win Thin, an emerging markets currency strategist at Brown Brothers Harriman in New York.
"So there is chatter about China, but a lot of this is month-end positioning. Emerging markets had a great run-up and some people are closing their books and taking profits."
Mexico's peso has gained around 3.5 percent this month as better-than-expected corporate earnings in the United States bolstered hopes for an economic recovery.
The peso briefly broke past a key resistance level of 13.30 per U.S. dollar that has held in recent session, but bounced back and traders said there were firm buy orders at 13.30.
Also hurting sentiment on Wednesday, data showed new orders for long-lasting U.S. manufactured goods notched their biggest decline in five months in June.
The U.S. downturn has driven Mexico into a deep recession, and the central bank on Wednesday revised downward its growth outlook, saying the economy could shrink as much as 7.5 percent this year. [ID:nN29285122]
The shift had little impact on markets, traders said, as the move aligns the central bank's outlook with private estimates that expect Mexico's recession to be the deepest since the Great Depression of the 1930s.
In debt trading, the government's benchmark 10-year peso bond MX10YT=RR fell 0.154 of a point in price, pushing its yield up 2 basis points to 8.11 percent. (Reporting by Michael O'Boyle; Editing by James Dalgleish)
