PRESS DIGEST - South Korean newspapers - June 9
2009-06-08 20:11:00.0
(Reuters) - The following is a summary of major South Korean newspapers on Tuesday, prepared by Reuters in Seoul. Reuters has not checked the stories and does not guarantee their accuracy.
A South Korean maker of fur clothing based in the joint industrial park in the North Korean city of Kaesong plans to completely withdraw, the first such case, as security concerns grow and orders drop amid heightened tension between the two Koreas, according to a government source.
The ruling party has tentatively decided to introduce a grace period for legislation that will require contract workers to either be employed full-time or dismissed after two years, fearing it might cause massive layoffs in July when many temporary workers reach the end of their contracts.
In line with central banks around the world moving toward ending their easing stances on inflation fears, the Bank of Korea is moving to absorb liquidity. The central bank issued a record amount of monetary stabilisation bonds in May while the government recently decided to stop its blanket support for small- and medium-sized companies.
