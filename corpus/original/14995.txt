US copper at 2-1/2-week high, data raise recovery hopes
2009-07-01 10:46:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures rose to a 2-1/2-week high early Wednesday after manufacturing data from China, Europe and the United States reinforced global economic recovery hope.

* Copper for September delivery HGU9 surged 8.15 cents, or 3.6 percent, to $2.3535 a lb by 10:40 a.m. EDT (1440 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Morning range from $2.2720 to $2.3690, a high for the benchmark contract dating back to June 15.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 6,017 lots by 9 a.m.
* Copper underpinned by signs of bottoming in manufacturing sector. Industrial growth outside of United States, particularly in China, supporting the market - Tom Hartman, broker with Altavest Worldwide Trading in Mission Viejo, California.
* China's manufacturing sector extended a steady recovery in June, adding to evidence across Asia that the regional economy is finally pulling out of a deep dive.
* The official purchasing managers' index (PMI) for June rose to 53.2 from 53.1 in May, consolidating for the fourth month in a row above the watershed mark of 50. [ID:nPEK138883]
* "Manufacturing sounds good. It seems like we're getting some progress in other areas of the economy outside of the housing black hole." - Jack Ablin, chief investment officer with Harris Private Bank in Chicago.
* U.S. construction spending fell 0.9 percent in May to the lowest rate in more than five years, while pending sales of previously owned U.S. homes rose slightly in May, the fourth straight monthly gain. [ID:nN30459920] [ID:nN01500737]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> fell 225 tonnes to 265,725 tonnes Wednesday, compared with levels around 500,000 tonnes in early April.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU held steady at 59,795 short tons as of Tuesday.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 last traded at $5,155 a tonne, up $185 from Tuesday's closing level. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
