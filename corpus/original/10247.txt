Weak dollar lifts U.S. copper to 2-wk high at open
2009-10-08 10:48:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures jumped more than 3 percent to a two-week high Thursday morning, driven by a weak dollar, outside market strength, and indications that demand from China will pick up after week-long holidays.

* Copper for December delivery HGZ9 up 7.45 cents, or 2.7 percent, to $2.8540 a lb by 10:36 a.m. EDT (1436 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Range from $2.7850 to $2.8750, the contract's loftiest level since Sept. 23.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 10,309 lots by 9 a.m.
* Seven-week high Wednesday in the Baltic Exchange's main sea freight index .BADI, which tracks rates to ship dry commodities, pointing to fresh Chinese demand. [ID:nL7708072]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks fell by 300 tonnes to 346,850 tonnes on Thursday, still holding at highest levels seen since May 2008. [LME/STX1]
* COMEX copper warehouse stocks jumped 514 short tons to 54,483 short tons as of Wednesday.
* Workers at Chilean state miner Codelco's Andina copper mine rejected an early contract offer before official negotiations start this month. [ID:nN08518226]
* LME copper for three-month delivery MCU3 at $6,257 a tonne, up $162 from Wednesday's kerb close. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Walter Bagley)
