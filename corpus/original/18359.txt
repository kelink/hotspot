U.S. copper ends slightly lower with stock indexes
2009-05-12 15:59:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures closed with a small loss on Tuesday, giving up early gains when investors sold copper with U.S. equities, both of which got hit by a bout of profit-taking and a drop in U.S. exports.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for July delivery HGN9 settled 0.25 cent lower at $2.0860 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* July copper continued in a sideways pattern with a slightly higher bias, maintaining support set on May 1.
* COMEX estimated closing copper futures volume at 19,022 lots, compared with 18,841 lots traded on Monday.
* Open interest increased by 1,448 lots to 106,689 contracts as of May 11.
* Selling in U.S. equity markets undermined copper's early gains, though support held - traders.
* "The most obvious reason for copper to be under pressure today was the selloff in equities. That's where you're getting the bulk of that weakness," said David Meger, metals analyst at Alaron Trading in Chicago.
* S&P 500 and Nasdaq indexes fell on profit-taking in financials and technology, while a drop in U.S. exports damped investor enthusiasm. [.N]
* Earlier, copper launched a strong rally when the dollar lost ground against the euro and the yen - traders.
* "We've passed the worst in the recession. We're not necessarily moving into growth yet, but at least the pace of deterioration has slowed," said Matt Esteve, a foreign exchange trader at Tempus Consulting in Washington. [USD/]
* Fears that China, world's largest consumer, may slow its buying also leaves it vulnerable to a selloff - Meger.
* China reported overnight that it imported more copper than ever before in April as traders rushed to capitalize on Beijing's efforts to stockpile metal and on growing demand. [nHKG272336]
* Zambia's copper cathode production rose 17 percent to 167,185 tonnes during the first quarter, compared with last year, the central bank said. [nWEB4406]
* In northern Chile, mine worker's at Xstrata's XTA.L Lomas Bayas copper deposit continued their strike, but agreed to resume wage talks with management this week. [ID:nN11185931]
* London Metal Exchange copper warehouse stocks tumbled 6,150 tonnes to 379,025 tonnes.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU rose by 684 short tons to 48,740 short tons as of Monday.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 ended Tuesday at $4,595 a tonne, up slightly from $4,565 at Monday's close. (Reporting by Carole Vaporean; Editing by Walter Bagley)
