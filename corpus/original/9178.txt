The company forecast a recurring profit of 40 billion yen for the full year to March 2010 compared with a consensus estimate of 47.9 billion yen profit, according to a poll of 18 analysts in Thomson Reuters I/B/E/S.
Asia's steelmakers are expected to see sequential earnings growth by the end of 2009, helped by the upturn in demand and lower input costs, although earnings momentum could slow in the current quarter due to oversupply in China.
But Japanese steel mills' profits will stay sharply lower than those of their Asian peers this financial year due to huge inventory writedowns, booked mostly in April-June, stemming from annual changes in raw materials costs. They adopt more stringent asset impairment accounting rules than their Asian counterparts.
Shares in JFE, which has strong exposure to Asia's export market, have risen 37 percent this year, sharply outperforming bigger rival Nippon Steel's 23 percent gain. (Reporting by Yuko Inoue; Editing by Valerie Lee)
