UPDATE 1-Russia, India to study rouble/rupee use in trade
2009-10-22 09:44:00.0
(Reuters) - Russia and India have agreed to study the possibility of using national currencies in bilateral trade, Russia's central bank said in a statement on Thursday.
The decision was taken at a meeting of the banking section of the Russia-India inter-governmental commission, it said.

Russia's trade turnover with India stood at only $3.9 billion in January-August 2009 compared with $36.5 billion in trade with China, and even lagged bilateral trade with Brazil at $4.4 billion.
Brazil, Russia, India and China make up the BRIC informal group of major emerging economies. Deputy Prime Minister Sergei Sobyanin said earlier this week Russia wants to boost its trade with India to $10 billion in 2010 and to $20 billion by 2015.
India's Trade Minister Anand Sharma told Reuters last month in Moscow that Russia's Prime Minister Vladimir Putin may visit India later this year but the Russian government's press service said it was not aware such visit is planned.
Russia has long called for less global dependence on the dollar, and has reduced the share of the U.S. currency in its $400 billion reserves to less than 50 percent.
With China, it has called for discussion on the possibility of creating a new supranational reserve unit, based on the International Monetary Fund's Special Drawing Rights (SDRs), although officials admit any such process would take years.
Big oil producing nations denied a British newspaper report earlier this month that Gulf Arab states were in secret talks with Russia, China, Japan and France to replace the dollar with a basket of currencies in trading oil. (Reporting by Gleb Bryanski; editing by Chris Pizzey)
