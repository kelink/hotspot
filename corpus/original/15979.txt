Strengthening dollar extends US copper correction
2009-06-15 10:27:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures lost more than 3 percent of their value early Monday, as further strength in the U.S. dollar pulled prices down in an extended correction from last week's charge to eight-month highs.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for July delivery HGN9 slumped 7.55 cents, or nearly 3.2 percent, to $2.2980 a lb by 10:22 a.m. EDT (1422 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 10,439 lots by 9 a.m.
* Copper weighed down by stronger tone in the dollar. Correction likely to run its course for a little while longer as many metals overextended on the upside - MF Global analyst, Edward Meir.
* The dollar rose in response to comments from Russia's Alexei Kudrin that the dollar's role as the world's main reserve currency is unlikely to change in the near future. [ID:nFCA000184]
* Economic data from U.S. showing New York's factory sector contracted at a more severe rate in June added to the industrial metal's bearish price action. [ID:nN15768]
* "We are particularly concerned that the rebound in Chinese demand has yet to show up in the rest of the world, meaning that China's critical export sector will continue to struggle." - Meir.
* China's May imports of unwrought copper and semi-finished copper products hit a fresh high for the fourth straight month on continued arbitrage trade. [ID:nHKG186125]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> shed another 3,300 tonnes to 286,975 tonnes on Monday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- slipped to 21,375 tonnes on Friday, from a previous tally of 24,725 tonnes.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU jumped 949 short tons to 59,472 short tons as of Friday.
* COMEX net speculative short position in copper futures grew to 18,769 lots in the week ended June 9, from 18,654 lots the previous week. Open interest dropped to 109,820 contracts open from 111,357 contracts in the week of June 2. [ID:nN1281505]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 last traded at $5,047 a tonne, down $183 from Friday's closing level. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Lisa Shumaker)
