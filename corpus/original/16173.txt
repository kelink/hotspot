US stock futures mixed as oil falls, dollar firms
Fri  EDT
* U.S. stock index futures pointed to a mixed open on Wall Street on Friday, with futures for the S&P 500 SPc1 up 0.2 percent, Dow Jones DJc1 futures up 0.1 percent and Nasdaq 100 NDc1 futures down 0.3 percent by 1000 GMT.
* Oil eased to $72 a barrel on Friday, pressured by a firmer dollar, but stronger-than-expected Chinese economic data helped support prices near an eight-month high. The market on Thursday settled at $72.68, the highest since Oct. 20 after a three-day rally, making it look overvalued to some analysts. [O/R]
* The dollar edged up against a basket of currencies on Friday, recovering as traders booked profits from the U.S. currency's slide earlier this week, while investors awaited a G8 finance ministers meet later in the day. [ID:nLC360095]
* European stocks were down 0.2 percent in morning trade, retreating from Thursday's five-month closing high, with influential commodity-related shares falling along with oil and metal prices. [.EU]
* Japan's Nikkei stock average
climbed to close above 10,000 on Friday for the first time in eight months as upbeat data from Japan and China fed hopes for global economic recovery, leading to broad-based buying. [.T]
* China's May factory output rose more than forecast and retail sales growth accelerated, giving new impetus to investor hopes that the world's third-largest economy can lead a global revival. [ID:nLC440656]
