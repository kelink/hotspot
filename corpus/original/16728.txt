), China's fourth-largest power company by market value, said it planned to issue up to HK$6.05 billion (US$775.9 million) in rights shares, raising money to enhance its capital base and to pay for future acquisitions.
The company said in a statement late on Thursday that it would issue up to 432.27 million rights shares in the proportion of one rights share for every 10 existing shares held at HK$14 per share, or 21.9 percent discount to the closing price of HK$17.92 on Thursday.
"The purpose of the rights issue is to strengthen the company's capital base, augment its overall financial position, and provide more flexibility to enable the company to capture any development and expansion opportunities which may arise in the future," executive director Wang Xiao Bin said in the statement.
The company, which will be added to the blue chip Hang Seng Index
from June 8, will increase investment in renewable energy such as contruction of wind farms and hydro-electric plants, and would continue to invest in large-scale efficient coal-fired generation units in China.
