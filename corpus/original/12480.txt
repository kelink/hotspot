"In the second half, driven by the policy of exploring domestic needs launched by Chinese government, the consumption of beer will continue to recover," Tsingtao said.
National beer output increased 6 percent year-on-year to 205.1 million hectolitres in the first half, in line with slower growth in China economy. Tsingtao said its sales volume increased 12.6 percent year-on-year to 30.2 hectolitres during the period.
Its six-month profit increased to 639.78 million yuan ($93.65 million), compared with 381.13 million yuan per year earlier, as its effort to strengthen plant management and lower costs helped boost its earnings and sales.
Last month, the Chinese brewer had said it expected its net profit to increase by 60-70 percent year-on-year in the first six months of 2009.
Improvement in brand equity and product mix, and as Tsingtao depleted its high-cost barley in first quarter and began to benefit from low-cost barley from second quarter also helped boost earnings.
