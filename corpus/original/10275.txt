PRESS DIGEST - South Korean newspapers - Oct 9
2009-10-08 19:57:00.0
(Reuters) - The following is a summary of major South Korean newspapers on Friday, prepared by Reuters in Seoul. Reuters has not checked the stories and does not guarantee their accuracy.
The government has moved up a plan to mass produce electric vehicles to 2011 from the prior target of 2013 to speed up preparations for environmental regulations and oil price hikes, the Knowledge Economy Ministry announced on Thursday.
The government budget to support research and development in small and medium-sized enterprises will be raised 14.4 percent to 560.7 billion won ($482.1 million) in 2010, the state-run Small and Medium Business Administration said on Thusday.
South Korea is considering dispatching 300 soldiers to Afghanistan to guard around 30 South Korean citizens who are working there providing medical and other non-military services, defence sources said Thursday. The government plans to increase the the reconstruction team to 85 people by early next year.
A Blue House spokesman said Thursday it welcomed the Japanese foreign minister's suggestion on Wednesday that South Korea, China and Japan write a common history textbook, but said it wouldn't be easy and could take a long time. ($1=1163.0 Won)
