UPDATE 1-Korea Life may raise around $2 bln in IPO-sources
2009-11-05 22:34:00.0
(Reuters) - Korea Life Insurance Co., the country's No. 2 life insurer, may raise around $2 billion through its public offering, sources with direct knowledge of the matter said, in what would be Korea's largest IPO since 2006.
Korea Life joins a rash of planned insurance offerings in Asia, as the industry seeks to expand and as rising stock markets lift earnings for life insurers, for whom investment returns are a key source of income.
The Asia insurance IPO list includes AIG's AIA, Shanghai-traded China Pacific Insurance (
), which aims to raise $3.5 billion in Hong Kong, and Reliance Life in India.
Sources on Friday pegged the value of Korea Life at roughly $10 billion, and the expectation is to float a fifth of the business.
