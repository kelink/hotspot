* Copper for July delivery HGN9 slipped 6.25 cents, or 2.6 percent, to $2.3825 a lb by 10:28 a.m. EDT (1428 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* On Thursday, U.S. dollar weakness, constructive import data from top metal consumer China, and upbeat U.S. retail sales and weekly jobless claims data drove benchmark July COMEX contract to an eight-month peak at $2.4575 a lb - analysts.
* China's May imports of unwrought copper and semi-finished copper products hit a fresh high for the fourth straight month on continued arbitrage trade. [ID:nHKG186125]
* China's production of refined copper fell 2 percent from April's second-highest level ever because of reduced scrap supplies, easing pressure on domestic stocks after record imports in the past four months. [ID:nPEK326641]
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 14,040 lots by 10 a.m.
* Copper down in profit-taking retreat from Thursday rally to eight-month high at $2.4575 a lb, with stronger tone in the U.S. dollar and general weakness in the broader commodity complex influencing early price direction - analysts.
* The Reuters-Jefferies CRB index .CRB, a global commodities benchmark, was trading down 0.7 percent near its early session low at 264.28.
* Industrial production in the 16-country euro zone plunged 21.6 percent in the year to April, a record fall that was steeper than economists' forecasts. [ID:nBRQ007375]
* The weaker-than-expected data drove the dollar up against the euro, and provided additional weight to dollar-priced copper as it makes the metal more expensive for non-U.S. investors. [USD/]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> went down by 2,900 tonnes to 290,275 tonnes on Friday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- fell to 24,725 tonnes on Thursday, down from 74,500 tonnes in May.
* Copper inventories in warehouses monitored by the Shanghai Futures Exchange rose 33 percent to 60,647 tonnes from 45,480 tonnes a week earlier. [ID:nBJD000881]
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU rose by 39 short tons to 58,523 short tons as of Thursday.
* Chile's Collahuasi copper mine, owned by global miners Xstrata Copper XTA.L and Anglo American (
), should reach an annual output of 1 million tonnes of copper by 2015. [ID:nN11525301]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 last traded at $5,235 a tonne, down $145 from Thursday's close. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Lisa Shumaker)
