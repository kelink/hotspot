Upbeat data lifts U.S. copper up from earlier lows
2009-10-05 10:45:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures reversed earlier losses and moved into positive territory Monday morning, after data showed the services sector expand in September following 11 straight months of contraction.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for December delivery HGZ9 rose 0.30 cent at $2.6845 a lb by 10:33 a.m. EDT (1433 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* While a consolidation or a bounce back up in the short term would not be surprising, a breach of $2.64 on a closing basis would be a bearish development that would open the way for $2.35 - CitiFX strategists.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at a thin 5,032 lots by 9 a.m.
* The Institute for Supply Management's non-manufacturing index for September came in at 50.9 compared with 48.4 in August, and was the first time in eleven months the index breached the 50 reading that separates contraction from growth. [ID:nWEN4364]
* Economic recovery concerns following Friday's weaker-than-expected September payrolls report limiting copper's upside - analysts.
* "It seems all markets are in the same boat now-- having discounted the end of the recession by moving sharply higher over the spring and summer months, they are now easing back, discouraged by the anemic pace of the widely anticipated recovery." - MF Global analyst, Edward Meir.
* Copper buoyed by U.S. dollar weakness after a meeting of Group of Seven finance chiefs reinforced the market's view policy-makers are comfortable with a gradually weakening American currency. [USD/]
* Copper additionally supported by 200 tonne decline in London stockpiles [LME/STX1] and a sharp 5,225 tonne rise in canceled warrants -- metal tagged for future delivery - analysts.
* COMEX copper warehouse stocks rose 69 short tons to 53,673 short tons as of Friday.
* Money managers held a net short position of nearly 4,000 copper futures contracts in the week ending Sept. 29, from a net long of 247 contracts the previous week. [ID:nN02327236]
* LME copper for three-month delivery MCU3 last traded at $5,895 a tonne, up $16 from Friday's closing level. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Marguerita Choy)
