US copper ends up with stocks ahead of housing data
2009-05-18 14:38:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures closed near their session highs on Monday, as a rally in equity markets and a weaker dollar helped boost investor appetite for risk ahead of key economic data from the housing sector on Tuesday.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for July delivery HGN9 rose 5.45 cents, or 2.7 percent, to settle at $2.0720 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 18,665 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Friday hit 20,168 lots.
* Open interest fell by 376 lots to 105,875 contracts open as of May 15.
* U.S. April housing starts expected at an annual rate of 520,000 units, up from 510,000 in March - Reuters poll. [ID:nN18347358]
* U.S. equities rose as solid results from No. 2 U.S. home improvement retailer, Lowe's Cos Inc (
) , spurred hopes the recession was easing and consumer spending stabilizing. [.N]
* Copper buoyed by weaker dollar .DXY, as improving risk appetite from rising equities and crude oil reduces demand for safe-haven currencies. [USD/]
* Copper market largely influenced by elusive prospects for global economic recovery and a lack of alternative demand from China, outside of the government buying programs - MF Global analyst, Edward Meir.
* Chinese copper imports in April jumped to a record high at 399,833 tonnes.
* Shanghai's copper inventories rose for a third straight week to 35,389 tonnes last Friday, up 7,699 tonnes from a week earlier. [ID:nSHA47830]
* Copper upside additionally fueled by a 4,250-tonne inventory withdrawal in London warehouse stocks. Inventories stand at their lowest level since early January at 353,550 tonnes. <LME/STX1>
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- fell to 59,850 tonnes from Friday's 61,300 tonnes.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU rose 72 short tons to 49,583 short tons as of Friday.
* COMEX copper noncommercial net short position grew to 18,739 lots in the week ending May 12, up slightly from the 18,538 lots recorded in the previous week. Open interest increased to 108,470 lots from 104,619 lots a week earlier. [ID:nN15297756]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 closed up $70 at $4,520 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Marguerita Choy)
