PRESS DIGEST - South Korean newspapers - May 29
2009-05-28 20:19:00.0
(Reuters) - The following is a summary of major South Korean newspapers on Friday, prepared by Reuters in Seoul. Reuters has not checked the stories and does not guarantee their accuracy.
North Korea did not launch missiles off its west coast as expected due to concerns about Chinese fishermen operating in the area, a military source said.
Over 1 million mourners have visited the southern city of Kimhae to pay respect to the late president Roh Moo-hyun, a Kimhae native, the city government said.
The Constitutional Court has ruled a law banning unregistered outdoor rallies constitutional, rejecting an appeal filed by a civic group who said it violated freedom of expression.

The government has scrapped a blueprint for a second supplementary budget and is instead focusing on longer-term economic policies such as enhancing labour market flexibility and developing service industries, according to financial authorities.
