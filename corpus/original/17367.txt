US copper gain early ground as data boosts risk
2009-05-28 11:15:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures rose at the open Thursday as investor appetite for risk grew after a batch of better-than-expected data pointed to more stability in the the world's largest economy.

* Copper for July delivery HGN9 up 3.00 cents at $2.1510 a lb by 11:09 a.m. EDT (1509 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 5,363 lots by 10 a.m.
* Copper underpinned by recent upbeat economic data pointing to a more stable picture in the housing sector and a more confident U.S. consumer - Bart Melek, Global Commodity Strategist with BMO Nesbitt Burns in Toronto.
* Sales of newly built U.S. single-family homes rose 0.3 percent to a 352,000 annual pace, from a downwardly revised 351,000 in March - Commerce Department. [ID:nN28225440]
* New orders for long-lasting U.S. manufactured goods rose 1.9 percent, the biggest percentage advance since December 2007, while a separate report showed fewer workers filed for new jobless benefits last week. [ID:nN28317740]
* LME stocks of copper dropped by 2,150 tonnes to 317,125 tonnes, while canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- stood at 43,650 from 43,375 tonnes.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU rose 205 short tons to 54,129 short tons as of Wednesday.
* High levels of Chinese copper scrap imports not a sign of healthy consumption and might present risks if global demand continues to be hit by the financial crisis - Lili Shi, an independent Chinese scrap metal consultant. [ID:nLR116800]
* Rising inventories of copper and slimming order books for fabricators signaling slowing demand from China, the world's top consumer of the metal. [ID:nHKG228088]
* China's State Reserves Bureau (SRB) reportedly sold off a small volume of its contracted copper imports over the past month, and might sell up to 50,000 tonnes. [ID:nPEK213195]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 was last traded at $4,700 a tonne, compared with Wednesday's last bid at $4,665 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
