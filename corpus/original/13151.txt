"The petroleum markets are backing off from the highs, seemingly prepared, along with the S&P 500, to at least take a break from the recent rally," said Tim Evans, analyst at Citi Futures Perspective.
Front-month September crude futures have dropped from the $68.99 high on Monday, the highest intraday since July 2's $69.74.
U.S. equities fell on the consumer confidence report, and combined with disappointing quarterly results, dampened hopes for a strong economic recovery. .N
* On the New York Mercantile Exchange, at 1:45 p.m EDT (1745 GMT), September crude was down $1.38, or 2.02 percent, at $67 a barrel, trading from $66.48 to $68.86.
* In London, September Brent crude fell $1.12, or 1.58 percent, to $69.69 a barrel, trading from $69.07 to $71.36.
* NYMEX August RBOB was down 2.47 cents, or 1.28 percent, at $1.91 a gallon, trading from $1.8960 to $1.9475.
* NYMEX August heating oil dropped 3.04 cents, or 1.69 percent, to $1.7662 a gallon, trading from $1.7575 to $1.8045.
* The September/September RBOB crack spread was at $12.13, after ending at $11.65 on Monday. The September/September heating oil crack spread was at $8.36, after ending at $8.11 on Monday.
* The spread between the current front month and the five-year forward crude contract was at $19.46, based on the September 2014 contract Monday settlement at $86.46. The spread ended Monday at $18.08.
