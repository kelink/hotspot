US copper ends lower, consolidates 7-1/2 mth peak
2009-06-02 14:30:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures drifted down in a tight band of consolidation on Tuesday, but remained well-supported near 7-1/2 month highs due in large part to a deteriorating dollar and upbeat economic data.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for July delivery HGN9 ended down 2.15 cents at $2.2975 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Session range from $2.28 to $2.3530, which marked a new high for the benchmark contract dating back to Oct. 14.
* Monday's breakout above multimonth congestion level at $2.24 (a lb) to fuel a trending move toward $2.40 - Ralph Preston, futures analyst with HeritageWestFutures.com in San Diego, California.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 20,290 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Monday hit 26,474 lots.
* Open interest rose by 141 lots to 110,544 contracts open as of June 1.
* Copper ends lower in mild profit-taking retreat from new 7-1/2 month high above $2.35 - Michael K. Smith, president of T & K Futures and Options Inc. in Port St. Lucie, Florida.
* Pending Home Sales Index, based on contracts signed in April, rose 6.7 percent in April to 90.3 from 84.6 in March -- the biggest monthly increase since October 2001 - The National Association of Realtors. [ID:nN02503338]
* COMEX copper prices have rallied more than 60 percent in 2009, driven largely by a major restocking process in China, the world's top copper consumer.
* A 5 percent rise in Chinese copper prices SCFc1 over the past two days has weakened domestic demand for spot metal further, driving spot prices below those for forward delivery for the first time since mid-April. [ID:nHKG190837]
* Zambia's state-run power utility is rationing electricity to its vital copper mines because a water weed, introduced from Brazil in the 1990s as a flower, has blocked flows into the biggest hydro power dam. [ID:nL2527519]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> shed another 2,750 tonnes on Tuesday, bringing total warehouse levels to 309,225 tonnes.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- increased to 40,175 tonnes from 36,700 tonnes the previous session.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU rose 161 short tons to 55,825 short tons as of Monday.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 ended $25 easier at $5,050 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly)
