Economic optimsim lifts US copper back above $2/lb
2009-04-29 14:01:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures rallied back up above the $2.00-a-lb level on Wednesday after a government report offered some glimmers of economic hope, even as it showed the economy contract at a surprisingly steep rate in the first quarter.

* Copper for July delivery HGN9 jumped 8.85 cents, or 4.6 percent, to settle at $2.0050 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Key upside target in benchmark July contract at $2.04-$2.05 area, followed monthly resistance point at around $2.25. First support pegged at $1.90, followed by $1.82-$1.86 area, and the 100-day moving average at around $1.64 - traders.
* COMEX estimated electronic futures volumes at 17,771 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Tuesday hit 24,684 lots.
* Open interest rose by 644 lots to 106,170 contracts open as of April 28.
* COMEX copper rallied after a Commerce Department report showed the U.S. economy shrank at a 6.1 percent annual rate in the first quarter, following a 6.3 percent contraction in the fourth quarter. [ID:nN28309952]
* "The (inventory) figures were better than people expected, so that's helping feed some optimism that had been somewhat shattered by the flu problem." - Steve Platt, futures analyst with Archer Financial Services in Chicago.
* Copper upbeat tone additionally fueled by a weaker dollar .DXY, stronger tone in equities, and continuous drawdowns in warehouse stocks levels - Platt.
* London Metal Exchange (LME) copper stocks fell by 8,825 tonnes to a three-month low at 411,450 tonnes on Wednesday. <LME/STX1>
* Canceled warrants -- metal tagged for delivery -- stood at 69,400 tonnes from 70,125 tonnes the day before.
* There were 8,125 tonnes of fresh cancellations on Tuesday, bringing the percentage of canceled warrants in the system up to 16.9 percent. The ratio of canceled warrants to total tonnage stands at its highest since early April 2008.
* COMEX copper stocks were unchanged at 48,056 short tons as of Tuesday.
* Treatment and refining charges paid to copper smelters in China to process foreign concentrates have fallen more than a third in the past six weeks on firm Chinese demand, but should rise next month as buying slows. [ID:nHKG188818]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 ended up $150 at $4,370 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Christian Wiessner)
