US copper ends lower, dollar rebound saps strength
2009-06-12 14:07:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures ended down on Friday, swept up in a dollar-induced sell-off across the broader commodity complex that pulled the price of the metal back down from the prior day's spike to an eight-month high above $2.45 a lb.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for July delivery HGN9 slumped 7.15 cents, or 2.9 percent, to settle at $2.3735 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* On Thursday, a weaker U.S. dollar, constructive import data from top metal consumer China, and upbeat U.S. retail sales and weekly jobless claims data drove benchmark July contract to an eight-month peak at $2.4575 a lb - analysts.
* China's May imports of unwrought copper and semi-finished copper products hit a fresh high for the fourth straight month on continued arbitrage trade. [ID:nHKG186125]
* China's production of refined copper fell 2 percent from April's second-highest level ever because of reduced scrap supplies, easing pressure on domestic stocks after record imports in the past four months. [ID:nPEK326641]
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 29,160 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Thursday at 38,103 lots.
* Open interest went up 1,784 lots to 115,130 contracts open as of June 11.
* Copper ends down in profit-taking retreat from weekly rally that drove prices up over 7 percent, with stronger tone in the dollar and general weakness in the broader commodity complex weighing on values - Sterling Smith, vice president with FuturesOne in Chicago.
* The Reuters-Jefferies CRB index .CRB, a global commodities benchmark, shed more than 1 percent and traded near a session low at 263.20 in afternoon business in New York.
* Copper's losses deepened as the dollar rose against the euro
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> went down by 2,900 tonnes to 290,275 tonnes on Friday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- fell to 24,725 tonnes on Thursday, down from 74,500 tonnes in May.
* Copper inventories in warehouses monitored by the Shanghai Futures Exchange rose 33 percent to 60,647 tonnes from 45,480 tonnes a week earlier. [ID:nBJD000881]
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU rose by 39 short tons to 58,523 short tons as of Thursday.
* Chile's Collahuasi copper mine, owned by global miners Xstrata Copper XTA.L and Anglo American (
), should reach an annual output of 1 million tonnes of copper by 2015. [ID:nN11525301]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 closed down $150 to $5,230 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by David Gregorio)
