US copper ends at 2-wk high, data boosts sentiment
2009-05-01 14:32:00.0
(Reuters) - Copper closed at a two-week high in New York futures trade on Friday, as a weaker dollar, falling inventories, and surprisingly strong data bolstered sentiment and raised expectations of a potential bottom in the economic downturn.

* Copper for July delivery HGN9 firmed 5.35 cents, or 2.6 percent, to settle at $2.1010 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX estimated electronic futures volumes at a slower-than-usual 10,285 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT) due to market holidays across Asia and Europe on Friday, and a holiday in Britain on Monday. [ID:nLS510015]
* Final volume on Thursday amounted to only 12,909 lots, while open interest rose by 687 lots to 104,636 contracts.
* Copper bulls encouraged by improved U.S. consumer confidence and a slower rate of contraction in U.S. manufacturing sector -- both indicative of a potential bottom in the economic downturn - Bart Melek, Global Commodity Strategist with BMO Nesbitt Burns in Toronto.
* Sentiment further bolstered by data showing expansion in China's manufacturing sector.
* China's official purchasing managers' index for April expanded to 53.5 from 52.4 in March, adding to tentative evidence that the world economy may be on the road to recovery. [ID:nPEK31266]
* London Metal Exchange (LME) copper stocks shed another 7,075 tonnes to 398,700 tonnes on Friday. Stockpiles have fallen 25 percent or 140,000 tonnes since late February. <LME/STX1>
* Canceled warrants -- metal tagged for delivery -- stood at 83,100 tonnes from 84,000 tonnes a day earlier, accounting for about 20 percent of total inventories.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU were unchanged at 48,056 short tons as of Thursday.
* Chile's Codelco, the world's top copper miner, produced 371,000 tonnes of the metal in the first quarter of 2009, up 6.9 percent from the same period last year. [ID:nN30112894]
* Mexican copper output fell to 18,923 tonnes in February, down 5.2 percent from the same month a year earlier.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 climbed $169 to settle at $4,600 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Marguerita Choy)
