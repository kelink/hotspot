US copper gains on dollar losses, trade data helps
2009-05-12 11:50:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures rallied on Tuesday, moving up sharply when the dollar lost ground to the euro and yen, with buying becoming more aggressive after reports that the U.S. trade deficit jumped.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for July delivery HGN9 was up 4.65 cents, or 2.23 percent, at $2.1350 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX by 10:50 a.m. EDT (1450 GMT).
* July copper continued moving in its sideways pattern, trading at the upper end of the recent range.
* COMEX put 1000 a.m. EDT futures volume at 9,001 lots.
* Dollar-denominated copper typically gets a boost in overseas markets, when the euro advances on the U.S. currency.
* "The jump in the trade deficit pushed dollar lower, increasing prices for energy and other commodities, which brought more interested investors to the metals," George Gero at RBC Wealth Management in New York.
* Kathy Lien, director of currency research at GFT Forex in New York, said the improvement in risk appetite is leading to a massive sell-off in the dollar across the board. [USD/]
* Meanwhile, China imported more copper than ever before in April as traders rushed to capitalize on Beijing's efforts to stockpile metal and on apparently growing demand, revived by a credit boom and increased state investments. [nHKG272336]
* Otherwise, Zambia's copper cathode production rose 17 percent to 167,185 tonnes during the first quarter of 2009 compared with the same period last year, the central bank said. [nWEB4406]
* In northern Chile, mine worker's at Xstrata's XTA.L Lomas Bayas copper deposit continued their work strike, but agreed to resume wage talks with management later this week. [ID:nN11185931]
* London Metal Exchange copper warehouse stocks tumbled another 6,150 tonnes to 379,025 tonnes.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU rose by 684 short tons to 48,740 short tons as of Monday.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 firmed to $4,636 per tonne, from $4,565 a tonne at Monday's close. (Reporting by Carole Vaporean; Editing by Christian Wiessner)
