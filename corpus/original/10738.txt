Weak economic data slam US copper down 3 percent
2009-10-01 14:33:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures closed sharply lower on Thursday, erasing nearly all of the previous session's gains, as risk appetite waned and the dollar strengthened after disappointing data from the labor and manufacturing markets.

* Copper for December delivery HGZ9 plunged 8.20 cents, or 2.9 percent, to settle at $2.7370 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 22,633 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Wednesday at 34,145 lots.
* Open interest increased by 2,412 lots to 115,936 contracts open as of Sept. 30.
* Copper down in sympathy with extended losses on Wall Street after the Institute for Supply Management's (ISM) manufacturing index disappointed investors with a reading of 52.6, from expectations for a reading of 54.0. [ID:nTAR001056]
* Disappointing ISM data combined with unexpected drop in durable goods orders last week fanning concern and emphasis on manufacturing sector - Matthew Zeman, head of trading with LaSalle Futures Group in Chicago.
* London Metal Exchange (LME) copper stocks added another 400 tonnes on Thursday, bringing total levels to the highest level since Mid-May at 346,050 tonnes. [LME/STX1]
* COMEX copper warehouse stocks dipped 162 short tons to 53,483 short tons as of Wednesday.
* LME copper for three-month delivery MCU3 closed down $170 at $5,985 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
