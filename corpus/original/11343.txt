FOREX-Yen up as shares drop; Bernanke 2nd term no surprise
2009-08-25 01:34:00.0
(Reuters) - The yen headed higher on Tuesday as share markets fell and investor interest in higher-yielding currencies ebbed, while currencies took in their stride news that Federal Reserve chief Ben Bernanke would be reappointed.
The dollar showed little reaction to the news as market participants had thought there was a good chance Bernanke would be given a second term to reassure financial markets as they recover from the global economic crisis, analysts said. [ID:nSP440422]
"I don't think there will be any major impact, but it should be positive for markets such as the stock and bond markets in the sense that an element of uncertainty has been removed," said Takahide Nagasaki, chief FX strategist at Daiwa Securities SMBC.
