Dollar weakness lifts US copper to early 1-mth high
2009-10-19 10:18:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures surged to a one-month high above $2.90 a lb Monday morning with the help of a weaker dollar, while a sense of cautious optimism from the London Metal Exchange Week provided additional underpinnings.

* Copper for December delivery HGZ9 climbed 6.50 cents, or 2.3 percent, to $2.9105 a lb by 10:13 a.m. EDT (1413 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Range from $2.8055 to $2.94, its highest level since Sept. 17.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 10,532 lots by 9 a.m.
* Dollar's collapse near the psychologically significant $1.50 mark against the euro undoubtedly lending an element of support to commodities in general - MF Global analyst Edward Meir.
