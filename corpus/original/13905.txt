The bank's outstanding loans used to fund flows of exports and imports reached 365.7 billion yuan at the end of June, up 42.7 percent from the level at the start of this year, it said in a statement on Thursday.
The World Trade Organisation has forecast a 10 percent fall in global trade volume in 2009, partly because of a big drop in the availability of financing to back trade. [ID:nLA344001]
To help fill the gap, China has relaxed rules to make it easier for banks to grant loans in support of goods shipments in and out of the country.
Its ratio of bad loans for trade finance stood at 0.2 percent at the end of June, lower than the bank's overall non-performing loan ratio, the statement added. ($1=6.830 Yuan) (Reporting by Langi Chiang and Simon Rabinovitch; Editing by Jon Loades-Carter)
