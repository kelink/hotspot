TIMELINE-Credit Suisse returns to profits
2009-04-23 10:32:00.0
(Reuters) - Credit Suisse's (
) first-quarter net profit far exceeded forecasts thanks to surprisingly robust trading gains and healthy rich client inflows, making it upbeat about its future while some other banks are still struggling.
1856 - The Schweizerische Kreditanstalt (SKA) founded by Alfred Escher, the predecessor to today's Credit Suisse, opens for business.
1964 - Credit Suisse is granted a license as a full-service bank, allowing it to take deposits and carry out all other types of banking in the USA.
1977 - The bank experiences a major scandal when authorities began investigating a fraudulent banking and foreign exchange trading scheme at the company's Chiasso branch involving more than $1.2 billion. The losses resulted in the resignation of several top executives.
2004 - Forms three business units: Credit Suisse, Credit Suisse First Boston and Winterthur.
2007 - Merger of the four independent Credit Suisse private banks, Clariden, Bank Leu, Bank Hofmann, and Banca di Gestione Patrimoniale to form Clariden Leu.
Nov. - Credit Suisse says its third-quarter profit at its investment bank is all but wiped out by writedowns, leading to a 31 percent fall in group net earnings to 1.3 billion Swiss francs ($1.12 billion).
Oct. - Raises about 10 billion Swiss francs, or about 12 percent of its outstanding equity, from investors. The Qatar Investment Company increases its stake in the shares of Credit Suisse to 8.9 percent, while Saudi conglomerate Olayan increases its stake to 3.6 percent.
Dec. - Cuts 650 jobs in Britain, equivalent to roughly 3 percent of its investment banking force of about 21,300.
Feb. 2009 - Unveils an annual net loss of 8.2 billion francs, its biggest-ever annual loss.
April - Posts a net profit of 2 billion Swiss francs ($1.71 billion) after its investment bank recorded trading revenues of 4.9 billion Swiss francs, triggering a 5-percent share rally.
