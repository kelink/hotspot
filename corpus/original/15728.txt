* Most-active copper for September delivery HGU9 rose 1.25 cents to close at $2.2820 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Overall trend clearly up, with recent price action indicative of a normal downward correction from over-heated levels - Matthew Zeman, head of trading with LaSalle Futures Group in Chicago.
* Last week, COMEX copper prices climbed to an eight-month high above $2.45 a lb.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 17,021 lots by noon EDT (1600 GMT). Final volume on Wednesday 31,155 lots.
* Open interest dropped 1,818 lots to 113,503 contracts open as of June 17.
* Economically sensitive copper supported by data showing improvement in the labor market, stronger signs of life in a slumping factory sector and a rise in leading economic indicators -- all suggesting an easing in the recession.
* Continuing U.S. jobless claims fell by 148,000 to a smaller-than-expected 6.69 million in the week to June 6, the largest one-week drop since November 2001. [ID:nN18383787]
* Upbeat economic data reduce safe-haven demand for U.S. dollar, making commodities denominated in the U.S. currency a more attractive buy for non-U.S. investors. [USD/]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> declined 1,575 tonnes to 281,600 tonnes on Thursday, compared with levels around 500,000 tonnes in February and March.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- continued to drop, standing at 17,125 tonnes on Wednesday, or a little more than 6 percent of total warehouse inventories.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU were up 255 short tons at 59,778 short tons as of Wednesday.
