-- The United States has agreed to loosen restrictions on the export of hi-tech goods to China and speed up its recognition of the nation's market economy, Vice-Premier Wang Qishan said after the China-US Strategic and Economic Dialogue.
-- Zhuo Lin, widow of China's late leader Deng Xiaoping, died in Beijing on Wednesday. She was 93 years old.
-- There are 51,000 people in China who are called "new aristocrats", according to a Hurun report. These people, mostly living in Beijing, Shanghai, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, Chengdu and Shenyang, possess personal wealth ranging from 33 million yuan ($5 million) to 87 million yuan ($13 million).
-- Police in Urumqi, capital of the Xinjiang Uighur autonomous region, have detained 253 more suspects in connection with the July 5 riot that left 197 people dead. Police said that most of the newly detained were turned in by people from several ethnic groups.
