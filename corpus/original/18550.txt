* Copper for July delivery HGN9 was down 0.70 cent at $2.1575 a lb by 10:35 a.m. EDT (1435 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Technical resistance at the $2.24 a lb April peak seen capping the morning advance - Frank McGhee, head precious metals trader with Integrated Brokerage Services LLC in Chicago.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 4,828 lots by 9 a.m.
* Government data showed the U.S. economy shed 539,000 jobs in April, below the 590,000 expected by economists and the 663,000 jobs lost in March. The jobless rate rose to 8.9 percent. [ID:nN08517208]
* Copper eases in muted reaction to jobs data. Profit-taking pause at week's end - McGhee.
* Surprisingly strong data from the housing, manufacturing and labor sectors of the economy indicate a potential bottom in the downturn and fuel inflationary expectations - Zachary Oxman, managing director with TrendMax Futures in Encinitas, California.
* Imports of refined copper to China, the world's top consumer of the metal, rose to a record high in March, as the inventories on the LME dropped by some 30,000 tonnes.
* London Metal Exchange copper warehouse stocks shed another 4,900 tonnes to 389,000 tonnes -- lowest level since mid-January.
* Canceled warrants -- material tagged for delivery -- were slightly lower at 72,925 tonnes from Thursday's 74,875 tonnes. <MCU/STX4>
* Copper inventories in warehouses monitored by the Shanghai Futures Exchange rose 45 percent to 27,690 tonnes from 19,064 tonnes a week earlier.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU were unchanged at 48,056 short tons as of Thursday.
* Zambia, Africa's top copper producer, picked a Chinese firm to run its Luanshya Copper Mines (LCM). [ID:nL81044519]
* Mine worker strike at Xstrata's XTA.L Lomas Bayas copper deposit in northern Chile now in its third day. [ID:nN07425585]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 unchanged at $4,715 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Marguerita Choy)
