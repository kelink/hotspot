BEIJING/SHANGHAI, July 22
-- China's exports are expected to fall by about 20 percent year-on-year in 2009 as a marked decrease in overseas demand is not seen changing in the second half of the year, said Huo Yunchun, deputy head of the Development Research Center of the State Council.
-- Official statistics showed that 484,799 share trading accounts were opened last week, the highest level since January 2008, reflecting active stock markets in China.

-- China's small and mid-sized commercial banks are speeding up the transfer of credit assets with low profitability to other financial institutions in order to focus on assets with higher profitability.
-- China's southern city of Shenzhen, near Hong Kong, lowered its reference monthly wage for workers for the first time in 10 years due to the slowdown in economic growth.
-- The Agricultural Bank of China's outstanding loans to small firms have reached 398 billion yuan, accounting for about 11.7 percent of its total loans to companies.
-- Chinese and Russian troops will start a five-day joint anti-terrorism drill on Wednesday, despite the loss of an aircraft on Sunday.
-- China may consider cutting its retail fuel prices at the end of this month, in line with a recent drop in international crude oil prices, industry insiders said.
-- The spot price of iron ore will climb in the second half of this year if the Japanese and South Korean economies recover, and this may force China to accept the 33-percent discount for full-year ore prices now being offered by global miners, industry experts said.
