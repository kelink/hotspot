Aug 14 (Reuters) - Taiwan President Ma Ying-jeou, under mounting pressure over his government's response to a devastating typhoon, estimated that more than 500 people had died in flooding and mudslides. [nSP438700]
Typhoon Morakot struck Taiwan a week ago, but the scale of the disaster only started to become clear this week after the storm had weakened over neighbouring China.
August 1996 - Typhoon Herb sweeps through Taiwan, killing at least 45 people.
July 2001 - Typhoon Toraji, one of the deadliest to strike Taiwan, kills about 200 people.
September 2001 -Typhoon Nari dumps record amounts of rain on Taiwan causing Taipei's worst-ever flooding and killing at least 100 people across the island.
September 2002 - Typhoon Sinlaku sweeps northern Taiwan with strong winds and heavy rain, forcing more than 1,000 people to evacuate their homes.
July 2004 - Typhoon Mindulle lashes heavy rain over Taiwan, triggering widespread flooding and killing at least 22 people. Another 14 people were missing and feared dead.
August 2004 - Typhoon Aere rips past Taiwan, killing 14 people in landslides and flooding.
July 2006 - Tropical storm Bilis kills more than 500 people in the Philippines, Taiwan and China in floods, landslides and other incidents.
August 2007 - Typhoon Sepat kills more than 30 people in China, Taiwan and the Philippines.
July 2008 - Typhoon Kalmaegi kills at least 20 people and causes extensive flooding, landslides and crop damage in the south and central part of Taiwan.
September 2008 - Typhoon Sinlaku pounds Taiwan, bringing torrential rains and triggering landslides in the central and northern part of the island, killing 12 people.
August 14, 2009 - Taiwan President Ma Ying-jeou says more than 500 people have died in flooding and mudslides triggered by Typhoon Morakot which struck the island a week ago.
