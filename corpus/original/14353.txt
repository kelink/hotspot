U.S. copper ends strong amid dollar-induced rally
2009-07-09 14:35:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures settled sharply higher on Thursday, buoyed by renewed weakness in the dollar and improved investor tolerance for risk following positive comments about the global economy from the International Monetary Fund.

* On Wednesday, the contract hit $2.1420, its lowest level since June 23.
