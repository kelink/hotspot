US copper cuts loss but ends down, stocks, oil fall
2009-08-17 15:49:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures trimmed steep losses by Monday's settlement on a late round of short-covering, but still closed lower as investors became more wary of risky assets and sold commodities across the board, keying off sharp declines in oil and stock prices, traders said.

* Copper for September delivery HGU9 finished down 6.55 cent, or 2.31 percent, at $2.7705 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Range extended down to $2.7265, a low from last Wednesday. The session high was $2.84 a lb.
* On Friday, September copper rose to a high at $2.9490, a peak dating back to late last September.
* COMEX estimated final copper volume at 28,745 lots, down from 35,983 lots in Friday's dealings.
* Open interest rose by 1,758 lots to 119,391 contracts open as of Aug. 14.
* In late business, some speculators returned to the market to cover short copper positions - traders.
* "We've got a little bit of short-covering coming back into the market. Industrial metals got significantly overbought late last week. A couple of reports came out that hit foreign and U.S. stock markets. So, we've had some technical selling against that," said Integrated Brokerage Services LLC's head precious metals trader, Frank McGhee in Chicago.
* Dollar gained on the euro as investors sought a less risky currency, undermining dollar-denominated copper's value in non-U.S. markets - traders.
* Oil's steep 2 percent decline hit copper as investors unwound commodities across the board - traders.
* Crude oil prices slid below $66 a barrel, their lowest level this month, as investors became more cautious about the pace of global economic recovery. [O/R]
* Moreover, data released in Japan showed its economy pulled out of recession and growth was in line with forecasts, but economists were wary as exports, the biggest contributor to second-quarter growth, may slow as the effect of stimulus measures wears off. [ID:nT212505]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks rose by 1,175 tonnes to 294,050 tonnes on Monday. [LME/STX1]
* COMEX copper warehouse stocks lost 93 short tons to 53,299 short tons as of Friday. [CMWSU]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 finished the Monday kerb sharply lower at $6,050 a tonne. It hit a fresh 10-1/2 month peak at $6,480 early on Friday. (Reporting by Carole Vaporean; Editing by Lisa Shumaker)
