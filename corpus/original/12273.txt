US copper dips on dlr bounce, China data slows move
2009-08-11 10:43:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures surrendered earlier gains and headed into negative territory Tuesday morning, pulled lower by a modest bounce in the dollar and down despite supportive trade data from China, the world's top copper consumer.

* Copper for September delivery HGU9 slipped 2.65 cents to $2.7440 a lb by 10:35 a.m. EDT (1435 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* On Monday, benchmark September contract hit a fresh 10-month peak at $2.8465.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 10,874 lots by 10 a.m.
* Slight bounce in the U.S. dollar behind weaker tone in broader commodity complex - Bart Melek, Global Commodity Strategist with BMO Nesbitt Burns in Toronto.
* Copper's losses limited by sense of improved economic and demand outlook from recent string of better-than-expected data from the U.S. and China, signaling worst of economic downturn has passed.
* China ended five months of record copper inflows in July, but imports of scrap surged by two-thirds and domestic production of refined copper continued to climb, indicating solid demand during the summer. [ID:nHKG338126]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks down 100 tonnes to 291,175 tonnes on Tuesday. [LME/STX1]
* COMEX copper warehouse stocks went up 63 short tons to 54,175 short tons as of Monday. [CMWSU]
* LME Copper for three-months delivery MCU3 last traded at $6,030 a tonne, down $105 from the close on Monday. when it hit a new 10-month high at $6,258. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Marguerita Choy)
