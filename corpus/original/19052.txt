PRESS DIGEST - Malaysia - May 4
2009-05-03 20:03:00.0
Track China's Leaders
Sun  EDT
-- The government has taken the first step towards reviewing the Internal Security Act (ISA) with the formation of a Law Reform Committee, said Deputy Minister in the Prime Minister's Department Liew Vui Keong.
-- Cagamas Bhd, the country's largest buyer of home loans, will issue a further 7.5 billion ringgit worth of bonds for the rest of this year, following the successful issuance of 4.5 billion ringgit in the first quarter, its Chief Executive Officer Steven Choy said.
-- Two US-based multinational corporations are setting up their business process outsourcing and shared services centres in the 100 million ringgit SunTech Tower in Bayan Lepas soon, a source said.
-- Naza Kia Sdn Bhd, the automotive arm of the Naza Group which distributes Kia vehicles in the country, expects its share of the local passenger car market to grow to 10 percent by the end of 2010, when it introduces several new models this year, Naza Kia corporate communications director Samson Anand George said.
-- The government has capped the number of foreign hypermarket licences at 107 until the end of this year, to address the issue of unused licences, the Domestic Trade and Consumer Affairs Ministry's secretary-general Mohd Zain Mohd Dom said.
-- A suspected al-Qaeda operative had been working in Kuala Lumpur for more than four years before his cover was blown recently when he was arrested in Germany, sources said.
-- After the third unsuccessful auction of Putra Place on April 16, the lenders of delisted Metroplex Bhd have applied to the Kuala Lumpur High Court for a fresh auction of the property, sources said.
-- BNP Paribas, which expects the Asian economy, ex-Japan and China, to contract 1.1 percent this year, does not see the much heralded "green shoots" as a sign of sustainable recovery, its chief Asia economist Richard Iley said.
-- With America's trading partners threatening possible retaliation over the government's recent "Buy American" Business Council representing scores of corporations in Malaysia, among others, has asked that "trade-restrictive clauses be left out", the Senior Director of the Council, Marc Mealy said.
-- Three new static mass thermal scanners have been installed - two at KL International Airport and another at the Low-Cost Carrier Terminal - to screen arriving passengers as Malaysia declared itself free of influenza A (H1N1) but remained vigilant, Health Minister Liow Tiong Lai said.
-- The Asian Development Bank is keen to share Malaysia's experience and expertise in helping member countries in generating economic growth and wellbeing of the people, Second Finance Minister Ahmad Husni Hanadzlah said.
