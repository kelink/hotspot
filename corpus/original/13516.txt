US copper futures finish strong in technical buying
2009-07-22 16:17:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures ended Wednesday with strong gains, pushing above $2.50 per lb for the first time since October after an early advance in equity markets sparked a rash of technical buying, traders and brokers said.

* Copper for September delivery HGU9 soared 7.45 cents, or 3.04 percent, to settle at $2.5255 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* In after hours trade, September copper rose as high as $2.5380, a level last seen in mid-October.
* COMEX estimated final futures volume at 23,331 lots.
* "We certainly have momentum buying and we are now looking like we have a good chart break out on the upside," said Sterling Smith, analyst at Country Hedging Inc in Inner Grove Heights, Minnesota.
* In the 2009 first half, China consumed 3.7 million tonnes of copper, up 48 percent from 2008, according to Reuters calculations. [ID:nSP467325]
