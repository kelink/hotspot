US STOCKS-Wall St drops as orders data, China spur caution
2009-07-29 10:50:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. stocks fell on Wednesday as a larger-than-expected drop in June new orders for long-lasting U.S. manufactured goods fanned fears of a weak economic recovery, and investors fretted about China's equities slide.
"If you look at our market, we had a bounce in a bear market. The recovery is still weak, but I don't think it's as bad as it once was forecast," said Dan Faretta, senior market strategist at Lind-Waldock, a retail brokerage firm, in Chicago.
