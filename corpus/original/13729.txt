TAIPEI, July 20
-- The speed skating and tug-of-war teams brought Taiwan two more gold medals at the World Games held in the island's southern city of Kaohsiung. The island has so far collected five golds, four silvers and two bronzes.
-- Taiwan will increase by the end of August its direct flights between Taipei and China's Qingdao city in Shandong province to 10 every week from four now.
-- Taiwan will revert to using the name Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall for the island's landmark tourist attraction, after the previous administration changed its name to the Taiwan Democracy Memorial Hall as part of its efforts to de-Sinify Taiwan.
-- Taiwan's cabinet is dissatisfied with the defence ministry's plan to increase its budget by T$90 billion ($2.7 billion) when it changes its policy to scrap mandatory military service and rely on recruitment, citing unidentified sources.
-- Tsai Ing-wen, chairwoman of Taiwan's main opposition Democratic Progressive Party, will send a petition to the Central Election Committee to hold a referendum on whether Taiwan should sign an FTA-like financial agreement with China, known as the Economic Cooperation Framework Agreement.
-- Inventec Group Chairman Yeh Kuo-i has invested T$2.4 billion ($73 million) in the Imperial Hotel Taipei, becoming the hotel's biggest shareholder as he beefs up investments in the island's tourism industry.
