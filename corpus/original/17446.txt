Confidence data, technicals drive US copper higher
2009-05-26 14:05:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures settled up near their session highs on Tuesday, after a jump in U.S. consumer confidence and strong technical momentum outweighed earlier concerns about waning demand signals from China.
For a detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for July delivery HGN9 climbed 4.35 cents, or 2.1 percent, to settle at $2.1410 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 18,395 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Friday reached 19,396 lots.
* Open interest rose 996 lots to 107,391 contracts open as of May 22.
* Confidence data and strong technical chart patterns driving forces behind copper's rally - Charles Nedoss, senior account manager and metals analyst with Peak Trading Group in Chicago.
* Demand worries from falling levels of canceled copper warrants and indications of slowing demand from China, the world's leading copper consumer, linger - analysts.
* China's State Reserves Bureau (SRB) reportedly sold off a small volume of its contracted copper imports over the past month, and might sell up to 50,000 tonnes. [ID:nPEK213195]
* Canceled warrants of copper -- material earmarked for delivery -- fell to 47,625 tonnes from 52,875 tonnes on May 21.
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> slipped 6,800 tonnes to 326,575 tonnes on Tuesday.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU went up another 561 short tons to 53,062 short tons as of Friday.
* COMEX net speculative short position in copper futures rose to 18,808 lots in the week ended May 19, from 18,739 lots a week earlier. Open interest was down at 105,791 contracts in the latest week, compared with 108,470 in the week of May 12. [ID:nN22391365]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 ended up $50 at $4,660 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly)
