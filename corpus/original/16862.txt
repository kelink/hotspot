US copper ends down, recovering dlr saps strength
2009-06-03 15:13:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures ended sharply lower on Wednesday, as the dollar corrected from recent losses and weaker-than-expected economic data prompted concerns about recovery hopes.

* Copper for July delivery HGN9 sank 8.55 cents, or 3.7 percent, to close at $2.2120 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* On Tuesday, the benchmark July contract climbed to a 7-1/2 month peak of $2.3530.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 26,617 lots by 1 p.m. (1700 GMT). Final volume on Tuesday reached 23,507 lots.
* Open interest rose by 813 lots to 111,357 contracts open as of June 2.
* Copper driven lower by recovering dollar and weaker equity markets after a batch of soft data stirs economic fears - David Meger, metals analyst with Alaron Trading.
* U.S. companies cut 532,000 jobs in May, more than economists had expected - ADP Employer Services. [ID:nN03546607]
* "We're expecting a consolidation/retracement ahead of Friday's jobs number in tandem with this new round of economic data that has prompted some concern about the recovery." - Meger.
* U.S. nonfarm payroll jobs are expected to fall by 520,000 in May, compared with a 539,000 drop in April - Reuters poll. [ID:nN02510310]
* U.S. service sector, which accounts for 80 percent of economic activity, contracted for the eighth straight month in May - The Institute for Supply Management. [ID:nWEN9983]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> fell by 2,700 tonnes to 306,525 tonnes on Wednesday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- declined to 37,550 tonnes from 40,175 tonnes the previous session.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU added 205 short tons on Tuesday, to total warehouse levels of 56,030 short tons.
* China's copper consumption is expected to grow 8 percent in 2009 because of robust demand from the cable industry, but supply is still abundant - Wang Zhongkui, deputy general manager of state-owned research firm Antaike. [ID:nSHA263977]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 was last quoted at $4,915 a tonne, from $5,050 on Tuesday. (Reporting by Chris Kelly)
