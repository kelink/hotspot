Futures dip after Spain warned on debt rating
2010-12-15 07:56:00.0
(Reuters) - Stock index futures edged lower on Wednesday after Spain was warned about its credit rating, rekindling fears about the euro-zone debt crisis.
* Credit rating agency Moody's warned Spain its debt rating could be downgraded and said it was concerned about the country's high funding needs.
* Investors awaited economic data, including the November Consumer Price Index (CPI), due at 8:30 a.m. EST (1330 GMT). Economists in a Reuters survey expect a 0.2 percent rise, unchanged from October.
* The New York Federal Reserve will release its Empire State Manufacturing Survey for December, also at 8:30 a.m.
* S&P 500 futures were down 2.5 points and slightly below fair value, a formula that evaluates pricing by taking into account interest rates, dividends and time to expiration on the contract. Dow Jones industrial average futures fell 19 points, while Nasdaq 100 futures dipped 7 points.
