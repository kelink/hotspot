Nokia CFO: no plans for subcontractor use in 2009
2009-07-16 10:57:00.0
(Reuters) - The world's top cellphone maker Nokia Oyj (
) does not plan to use subcontractors this year as the demand for handsets has dropped, its Chief Financial Officer Rick Simonson told Reuters on Thursday.
Nokia said in March it had fully stopped using subcontractors in manufacturing of its mobile phone engines, which include the phone and software that enable its basic operations. [nLQ663777]
"I don't expect this to change based on what we expect for the rest of the year. Volumes for the rest of the year we can handle on our own," Simonson said in an interview.
Nokia's key subcontractors have been Foxconn (
), China's BYD (
), Jabil Circuit (
Nokia cut its handset units' profit margin and market share forecasts for the rest of 2009 on Thursday, sending its shares 14 percent lower. [nLG204714]
The overall cellphone market is expected to contract by about 10 percent this year, hurt as consumers rein in spending and handset sellers try to clear out unsold phones.
In 2008, Nokia outsourced about 17 percent of the manufacturing volume of its mobile phone engines, which include the phone and software that enable its basic operations.
