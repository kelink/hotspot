Brazil stocks fall to two-month low; real gains
2009-07-14 17:45:00.0
(Reuters) - Brazilian stocks fell to a two-month low on Tuesday as investors sold off shares of mining company Vale, energy giant Petrobras and other commodity-linked companies on concerns over the strength of the global economic recovery.

"It's clear that it's profit-taking," said Andre Hanna Farath, an analyst at the Interfloat brokerage.
Brazil's currency, the real BRBY, gained 0.56 percent to 1.97 reais per dollar.
Retail sales data in Brazil on Tuesday showed volumes grew 0.8 percent in May from April, ahead of expectations, but gloom in the United States proved the motivating factor for many investors.
Investor's Business Daily and TechnoMetrica Market Intelligence said their IBD/TIPP Economic Optimism Index dropped to 46.3 in July from 50.8 in June, reflecting pessimism in the United States among consumers battered by job losses and wary of the effectiveness of government interventions. Analysts expect policy-makers to reduce the benchmark interest rate, the Selic, even further from its current record low of 9.25 percent, although the bank has signaled rate cuts may be nearing an end.
