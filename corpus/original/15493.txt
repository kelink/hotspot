US copper bounces from 3-week low as dollar slides
2009-06-23 10:35:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures climbed from a three-week low Tuesday morning, with renewed weakness in the U.S. dollar helping to fuel an early bounce from Monday's precipitous decline.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for September delivery HGU9 firmed 4.40 cents to $2.1880 a lb by 10:18 a.m. EDT (1418 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Morning range from $2.2030 to $2.1235, a new low for the benchmark September contract since May 28.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 9,301 lots by 9 a.m.
* "Overall it's weaker than expected, but does show we're trying to carve out a bottom here. The foreclosure portion of sales is slowing down at least. One month's supply for single family homes is back down to the low for the year, so that's also a welcome sign. I don't know that it's a green shoot. It seems to be more of a stabilization in the housing market." - Jacob Oubina, currency strategist with Forex.com in Bedminster, New Jersey.
* On Monday, COMEX copper prices lost more than 5 percent amid economic worries and market speculation that China, the world's top copper consumer, will curtail near-term purchases of the metal.
* China's imports of refined copper hit a record 337,230 tonnes in May, but apparent consumption fell 3.5 percent from the previous month, reflecting increased stockpiles and signaling lower inflows ahead. [ID:nHKG80221]
* The World Bank said prospects for the global economy remain "unusually uncertain," despite recent signs of improvement in parts of the world and cut its 2009 growth forecasts for most economies. [ID:nSGP000012]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> declined 1,325 tonnes to 276,675 tonnes on Tuesday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave LME warehouses -- rose to 17,750 tonnes as of Monday, versus the prior day's total of 16,625 tonnes.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU stood even at 60,225 short tons as of Monday.
* World copper market in a surplus of 48,000 tonnes in first quarter - International Copper Study Group. [ID:nN22512379]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 traded at $4,820 a tonne, up $59 from Monday's close. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Walter Bagley)
