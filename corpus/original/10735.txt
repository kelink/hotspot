US copper futures weaken after ISM data lift dollar
2009-10-01 11:02:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures fell under the weight of currency-related selling and waning risk appetite Thursday morning, after data showed a key manufacturing index for September came in lower than expected.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for December delivery HGZ9 slipped 4.70 cents, or 1.7 percent, to $2.7720 a lb by 10:54 a.m. EDT (1454 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 9,616 lots by 10 a.m., due in part to week-long holiday in China. CN-HOLIDAYS
* Copper down from overnight highs after data showed China's manufacturing sector expand at its strongest rate in 17 months in September. [ID:nPEK178107]
* Dollar-induced selling hits copper after the Institute for Supply Management's manufacturing index hit 52.6, down from expectations for a reading of 54.0. [ID:nTAR001056]
* "It was pretty close to expectations, but if you're looking at recovery, the number really didn't do the trick. The orders were up and the headline number was up, and the employment component was lower again, so I think that's taken some air out of the recovery scenario." - Ronald Simpson, managing director, global currency analysis, with Action Economics in Tampa, Florida.
* The International Monetary Fund raised its 2010 global growth forecast on Thursday, as factory activity picked up across Asia and Europe, and U.S. consumer spending accelerated in August to its fastest pace in nearly eight years. [ID:nSP546135]
* London Metal Exchange (LME) copper stocks added another 400 tonnes on Thursday, bringing total levels to the highest level since Mid-May at 346,050 tonnes. [LME/STX1]
* COMEX copper warehouse stocks dipped 162 short tons to 53,483 short tons as of Wednesday.
* LME copper for three-month delivery MCU3 last traded at $6,085 a tonne, down $70 from Wednesday close. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
