Lower dollar lifts U.S. copper to multi-week highs
2009-10-19 14:24:00.0
(Reuters) - The price of copper shot up to a 5-1/2-week high above $2.95 a lb in New York futures trade on Monday, driven by strong investment money flows and currency-related buying as the dollar remained depressed and near 14-month lows against the euro.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for December delivery HGZ9 rallied 12.10 cents, or 4.3 percent, to settle at $2.9665 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Range from $2.8055 to $2.9690, its loftiest level since Sept. 9.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 31,591 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Friday at 21,353 lots.
* Open interest down 323 lots to 123,341 contracts open as of Oct. 16.
* Copper running on money flows and outside market influences, with general feeling of prolonged dollar weakness providing underlying support - Bill O'Neill, partner of LOGIC Advisors in Upper Saddle River, New Jersey.
* Bullish momentum/depressed dollar to boost copper up through psychological $3.00 level this week - Ralph Preston, futures analyst with HeritageWestFutures.com in San Diego, California.
