PRESS DIGEST - South Korean newspapers - May 18
2009-05-17 20:14:00.0
(Reuters) - The following is a summary of major South Korean newspapers on Monday, prepared by Reuters in Seoul. Reuters has not checked the stories and does not guarantee their accuracy.
The government recently drew up contingency plans in case of the abrupt closure of the Kaeseong industrial park in North Korea, with the top priority being the protection of South Koreans staying there, and is also considering raising insurance payments to companies who have invested there to 7 billion won ($5.59 million) from the current 5 billion won if the park shuts down.
South Korea's National Assembly Research Service estimated that the shutdown of the joint industrial park in Kaesong, North Korea would cost South Korea 1.36 trillion won in losses.
