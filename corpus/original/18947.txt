ADR Report-ADRs rise on optimism over U.S. bank stress tests
2009-05-04 14:13:00.0
(Reuters)- Overseas shares traded in the United States rose on Monday as investors bet government stress tests won't force banks to raise as much capital as originally thought. Stronger-than-expected U.S. housing data also lifted sentiment.
The Bank of New York Mellon's index of leading American Depositary Receipts (ADRs) .BKADR gained 3.5 percent to 105.09 while the U.S. benchmark S&P 500 index
added 2.1 percent to 8,386.76.
The Bank of New York Mellon's index of leading Asian ADRs .BKAS surged 4.5 percent to 107.01. Chinese stocks advanced to a nine-month closing high, helped by data that suggested its manufacturing sector was improving. [ID:nSHA6324] Hong Kong shares rose to a 6 1/2 month high on data from China and investor hopes the U.S. economy was stabilizing. [ID:nHKG56156].
