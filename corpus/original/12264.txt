Recovery doubts drag U.S. copper futures lower
2009-08-11 14:12:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures ended down near their session lows on Tuesday, as doubts about economic recovery resurfaced in response to mixed economic data from the United States and China, the world's top copper consumer.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for September delivery HGU9 fell 3.40 cents, or 1.2 percent, to settle at $2.7365 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* On Monday, benchmark September contract hit a 10-month peak at $2.8465.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 23,844 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Monday hit 36,688 lots.
* Open interest fell by 2,249 lots to 119,130 contracts open as of Aug. 10.
* Chinese copper imports fell nearly 15 percent to 406,612 tonnes in July from June. [ID:nHKG338126]
* Copper's losses deepened after a sharp 1.7 percent drop in U.S. wholesale inventories during June suggested businesses remained skeptical that demand has returned. [ID:nN11520426]
* Economy not recovering as quickly as everyone thinks it is. Financial Armageddon likely done, but less-poor data indicates economy not out of the woods - Zachary Oxman, managing director with TrendMax Futures in Encinitas, California.
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks down 100 tonnes to 291,175 tonnes on Tuesday. [LME/STX1]
* COMEX copper warehouse stocks up 63 short tons to 54,175 short tons as of Monday. [CMWSU]
* LME Copper for three-months delivery MCU3 ended down $105 at $6,030 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly)
