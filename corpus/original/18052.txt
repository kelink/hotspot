US copper up with Wall St, China demand fears cap
2009-05-18 10:49:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures edged higher early Monday, with a firmer open on Wall Street helping to support the advance, but economic concerns and worries about overhanging supply in China, the world's top copper consumer, limited the move.

* Copper for July delivery HGN9 firmed 2.25 cents to $2.04 a lb by 10:38 a.m. EDT (1438 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 8,247 lots by 10 a.m.
* Copper buoyed by slightly weaker dollar .DXY, as improving risk appetite from rising equity markets reduces demand for safe-haven currencies. [USD/]
* Copper being influenced by elusive prospects for global economic recovery and a lack of alternative demand from China, outside of the government buying programs - MF Global analyst, Edward Meir.
* China's April copper imports hit a record high at 399,833 tonnes.
* Shanghai's copper inventories rose for a third straight week to 35,389 tonnes last Friday, up 7,699 tonnes from a week earlier. [ID:nSHA47830]
* A 4,250-tonne inventory withdrawal in London warehouse stocks limiting further downside.
* LME stockpiles stood at their lowest level since early January, at 353,550 tonnes. <LME/STX1>
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- fell to 59,850 tonnes from Friday's 61,300 tonnes.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU rose 72 short tons to 49,583 short tons as of Friday.
* COMEX copper noncommercial net short position grew to 18,739 lots in the week ending May 12, up slightly from the 18,538 lots recorded in the previous week. Open interest increased to 108,470 lots from 104,619 lots a week earlier. [ID:nN15297756]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 was last trading up $33 at $4,483 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Christian Wiessner)
