US copper ends firm as dollar slides, data uplifts
2009-10-05 14:38:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures closed higher on Monday, as the dollar dipped and economic sentiment improved after data showed the U.S. services sector expand in September for the first time since August 2008.

* Copper for December delivery HGZ9 firmed 4.55 cents, or 1.7 percent, to settle at $2.7270 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at a thin 16,850 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Friday hit 25,498 lots.
* Open interest down 1,178 lots at 113,907 contracts open as of Oct. 2.
* Data reflects improved conditions in consumer-oriented services - Bart Melek, Global Commodity Strategist with BMO Nesbitt Burns in Toronto.
* The dollar fell against major currencies after a meeting of Group of Seven finance chiefs reinforced the market's view policy-makers are comfortable with a gradually weakening greenback. [USD/]
* Copper additionally supported by 200 tonne decline in London stockpiles [LME/STX1] and a sharp 5,225 tonne rise in canceled warrants -- metal tagged for future delivery - analysts.
* COMEX copper warehouse stocks rose 69 short tons to 53,673 short tons as of Friday.
* Money managers held a net short position of nearly 4,000 copper futures contracts in the week ending Sept. 29, from a net long of 247 contracts the previous week. [ID:nN02327236]
* LME copper for three-month delivery MCU3 ended up $41 at $5,920 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
