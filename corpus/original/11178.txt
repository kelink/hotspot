US copper ends up, capped by China demand concerns
2009-08-26 14:27:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures ended up a fraction on Wednesday, with surprisingly strong new-home-sales data providing some buoyancy to values, but a firmer dollar and concerns about Chinese demand capped the advance, analysts said.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Benchmark copper for December delivery HGZ9 rose 0.35 cent to settle at $2.8770 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 27,305 lots by 12 p.m. EDT (1600 GMT). Final volume on Tuesday hit 27,191 lots.
* Open interest up 909 lots to 118,454 contracts open as of Aug. 25.
* Copper sentiment weighed down by news of Chinese constraints on industrial over-capacity - Bill O'Neill, partner of LOGIC Advisors in Upper Saddle River, New Jersey.
* "If this is an indication that China is in there tweaking their monetary policy, this may potentially slow down expansion and serve as a negative for the market." - O'Neill.
* China's State Council said it would restrict lending, licenses and land supply, among other measures, to restrain excessive construction in the steel, cement, plate glass, coal chemical, silicon, wind power equipment and other sectors. [ID:nPEK157466]
* Safe-haven dollar-buying from a less-than-upbeat U.S. durable goods report seen eroding investor appetite for "riskier" assets, like copper. [ID:nCAT002814]
* London Metal Exchange (LME) warehouse stocks down 200 tonnes to 296,400 tonnes on Wednesday. [LME/STX1]
* COMEX copper warehouse stocks up 202 short tons to 52,686 short tons as of Tuesday. [CMWSU]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 closed down $20 at $6,291 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; editing by Jim Marshall)
