US copper climbs to 1-wk high on economic outlook
2009-05-01 11:15:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures climbed to their highest level in a week early Friday, buoyed by a weaker dollar, outflows of the metal from London warehouses and better-than-expected economic data.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for July delivery HGN9 was trading up 3.10 cents, or 1.5 percent, to $2.0785 a lb by 11:02 a.m. EDT (1502 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Morning range from $2.0375 to $2.09, its highest level since April 23.
* COMEX estimated electronic futures volumes at a thin 4,193 lots by 10 a.m., slowed by market holidays across Asia and Europe on Friday and a holiday in Britain on Monday. [ID:nLS510015]
* COMEX copper strengthened as China's official purchasing managers' index for April expanded to 53.5 from 52.4 in March, the fifth straight rise off a record low 38.8 in November. [ID:nPEK31266]
* A surprisingly sharp jump in U.S. consumer confidence in April and a less severe contraction in the U.S. factory sector suggested a potential bottom in the recession - Bart Melek, Global Commodity Strategist with BMO Nesbitt Burns in Toronto.
* London Metal Exchange (LME) copper stocks shed another 7,075 tonnes to 398,700 tonnes on Friday. Stockpiles have fallen 25 percent or 140,000 tonnes since late February. <LME/STX1>
* Canceled warrants -- metal tagged for delivery -- stood at 83,100 tonnes from 84,000 tonnes a day earlier, accounting for about 20 percent of total inventories.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU were unchanged at 48,056 short tons as of Thursday.

* Chile's Codelco, the world's top copper miner, produced 371,000 tonnes of the metal in the first quarter of 2009, up 6.9 percent from the same period last year. [ID:nN30112894]
* Mexican copper output fell to 18,923 tonnes in February, down 5.2 percent from the same month a year earlier.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 last traded at $4,545 a tonne, up $114 from Thursday's close. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by David Gregorio)
