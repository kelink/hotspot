US copper steadies at open, equities offer support
2009-07-21 09:03:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures steadied at the open Tuesday, consolidating the previous session's rally to 9-month highs and digesting another batch of quarterly corporate results.

* Copper for September delivery HGU9 edged up 0.30 cent to $2.4720 a lb by 8:57 a.m. EDT (1257 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* On Monday, the benchmark contract rallied to its highest level since October 14, at $2.4895.
* Copper supported by positive tone in equities, where shares of Caterpillar were up 9 percent in early trade after the machinery maker reported second-quarter earnings and raised its full-year outlook. [ID:nN21196202]
* Freeport-McMoRan Copper & Gold's (
) second-quarter profit dropped sharply as the recession kept copper demand low, even as the company mined higher grade ore at its vast Grasberg gold mine in Indonesia. [ID:nN21527523]
* Copper's upside limited by a slightly firmer dollar, which cut recent losses ahead of Federal Reserve chairman Ben Bernanke's congressional testimony later in the day. [USD/]
* Bernanke starts his twice-yearly testimony on the Fed's economic outlook and monetary policy before the U.S. House Financial Services Committee at 10 a.m. EDT.
* Economic outlook upbeat after an index gauging the U.S. economy's prospects rose for a third straight month in June, suggesting the recession was drawing to a close - New York-based Conference Board [ID:nN20360679]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> up 475 tonnes at 266,425 tonnes on Tuesday.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU down 132 short tons at 59,119 short tons as of Monday.
* World refined copper consumption exceeded production by 37,000 tonnes between January and April this year, versus a deficit of 147,000 tonnes in the same year-ago period - the International Copper Study Group (ICSG). [ID:nN20129704]
* China's imports of refined and alloy copper rose to 402,544 tonnes in June, from May's 354,567 tonnes, official customs data showed on Tuesday. [ID:nHKG122823]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 last traded at $5,415 a tonne, near Monday's 9-month high at $5,464.75. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
