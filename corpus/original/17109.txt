Brazil stocks, real jump on US, China factory data
2009-06-01 11:04:00.0
(Reuters) - Brazil's stocks and currency jumped on Monday after data showed signs of expansion in manufacturing in the United States and China, sparking optimism that the worst of the global recession is over.
The real BRBY hovered around its highest in eight months, jumping 1.7 percent to 1.942 reais to the dollar.

Brazil's currency, which has surged 20 percent so far in 2009, is expected to move in line with global market sentiment, traders and analysts said.
"This is making it particularly hard to predict short-term movements," said Enrique Alvarez, head of research at New York-based IDEAglobal, adding that the real might fluctuate between 1.92 and 1.96 reais to the dollar in coming days.
China said manufacturing expanded for a third month in May, triggering a surge in global stocks. Optimism that China might help pull the world economy from its steepest recession since World War II also sent commodity prices higher.
"The news flow is good, in spite of the tight conditions that still persist in developed markets," said Helena Biasotto, who helps manage 5 billion reais in assets for Banrisul in the southern city of Porto Alegre.
