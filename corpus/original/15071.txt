Strong dollar, weak confidence hits US copper early
2009-06-30 10:47:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures fell back from a new two-week high Tuesday morning, as the dollar extended gains against the euro after data showed U.S. consumer confidence fell in June after two straight months of gains.

* Copper for September delivery HGU9 slipped 5.80 cents, or 2.5 percent, to $2.2680 a lb by 10:36 a.m. EDT (1436 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Morning range from $2.2675 to $2.3650, a new high for the benchmark contract dating back to June 15.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at a hefty 19,640 lots by 10 a.m.
* Copper's morning losses tied to upbeat tone in the dollar after data showed a smaller-than-expected dip in U.S. home prices in April and a larger-than-expected drop in U.S. consumer confidence in June. [ID:nNYS005189] [ID:nNYS005191]
* Copper up nearly 65 percent in the first half of the year, but expected to meet renewed resistance in the third quarter due to a more stable dollar. [ID:nN30439427]
* Further pressure seen from a halted restocking program in China, the world's largest copper consumer. [ID:nPEK124145]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> shed another 1,350 tonnes to 265,950 tonnes on Tuesday.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU stood even at 59,795 short tons as of Monday.
* Chile's industrial production plunged 10.5 percent in May from the same month a year earlier, while the country's copper production fell 2.3 percent in May from a year ago.
* Chile is the world's top producer of the red metal.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 was last at $5,000 a tonne, down from $5,095 at the close on Monday. (Reporting by Chris Kelly)
