HK shares ease after rally; China stocks extend gains
2009-06-02 01:07:00.0
(Reuters) - Signs of economic recovery, government support steps and ample liquidity continued to bolster the Chinese market on Tuesday, but shares in Hong Kong pulled back as investors deemed some blue chips expensive after the recent rally.
Bank shares led losses in Hong Kong, with Chinese lenders slipping after Goldman Sachs sold a part of its holding in ICBC while global lender HSBC took a breather following the 25 percent run-up in its stock in May.
