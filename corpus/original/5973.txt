China runs stress tests on property trusts: report
2010-07-24 03:41:00.0
(Reuters) - China's banking regulator has run stress tests in the country's trust firms to see if they can withstand a downturn in the property sector, the Economic Observer reported on Saturday, citing unidentified industry sources.
Trust firms are required to submit information about their exposure to the real estate sector, including investment sizes, collaterals and risk control measures, to the China Banking Regulatory Commission (CBRC), the newspaper reported.
In the first half of this year, Chinese trust firms issued property trust investment plans totaling 67.7 billion yuan (nearly $10 billion), compared to 40 billion yuan for the whole of 2009, the newspaper reported.
In comparison, bank loans went to property developments were 442.3 billion yuan in the first half, the People's Bank of China said.
(Reporting by Zhou Xin and Alan Wheatley; Editing by Ron Popeski)
