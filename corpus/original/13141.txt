BANGKOK, July 29
- The government will contest in court an arbitration body's resolution requiring it to pay 1.4 billion baht ($41 million) compensation to the Don Muang Tollway, 10 percent owned by Germany's Walter Bau WTBG.DE. (THE NATION)
- National Police chief General Patcharawat Wongsuwan is likely to face a criminal charge in connection with last October's bloody carackdown on anti-government protesters, a source said (THE NATION)
- U.S.-based Western Digital WDC.N, the world's second-largest manufacturer of hard disk drives, is hiring 5,000 people in Thailand as the global demand for data-storage products has risen beyond expectations (BANGKOK POST)
- The Small and Medium Enterprise Development Bank may extend loans to joint ventures or other business set-ups between local and Japanese firms, Deputy Finance Minister Pradit Pataraprasit said (THE NATION)
- Petch Thai Pattana, developer of the Thai Diamond City Industrial Estate in Phetchaburi, has entered into joint-venture agreements with two residential companies in China to develop hotel and residential projects for Chinese retirees on the estate (THE NATION)
- Telecoms group Samart SAMA.BK is confident about meeting this year's consolidated revenue target of 23 billion baht despite the declining economy (THE NATION)
- Kasikorn Securities KBAN.BK is negotiating with three foreign securities firms to share their research and business cross-selling in foreign markets in order to support the Thai broker's goal of becoming a leader in the domestic equity market (THE NATION)
- TSFC Securities, the largest casualty in the Thai capital market from last year's global financial crisis, has reopened for business after a seven-month stoppage, according to Chairman Pakorn Malakul Na Ayudhya (BANGKOK POST)
