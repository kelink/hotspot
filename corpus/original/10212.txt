The Pacific markets were driving global demand for coal and were expected to achieve 5-10 percent compound annual growth over the next five or more years, Gregory H. Boyce, Peabody's chairman and chief executive said in a statement on the company's website.
"The company's presence in Jakarta expands our ability to serve India, China and other key Asian markets," Boyce said.
The company had said in June, it was actively pursuing investments in the Asia Pacific region, particularly in Indonesia, to boost production and trading capacity of thermal coal in the region. [ID:nJAK441949]
Peabody Energy was among companies that have previously expressed interest to buy a majority stake in Indonesia's fifth-largest coal miner PT Berau Coal.[ID:nHKG293444]
The St. Louis-based company is the world's largest private sector coal company, with 2008 sales of 256 million tonnes and $6.6 billion in revenues.
Indonesia is the world's top thermal coal exporters with coal production is expected to reach 250-258 million tonnes of coal this year, the Indonesian Coal Producers Association has said.
