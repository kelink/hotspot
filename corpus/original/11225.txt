Under current regulations, insurers may invest eight percent of their assets in infrastructure-related debt and equity.
In materials handed to reporters ahead of a briefing on its interim results, released on Tuesday, China Life said it would put greater emphasis on market timing and strategic planning in managing its equity portfolio.
As for its fixed-income investments, China Life said it would continue to adjust its assets in line with requirements while closely following market movements.
China Life posted a 15 percent rise in first-half profits, but it missed forecasts as a large decline in dividend income reduced net investment income by 25 percent.
