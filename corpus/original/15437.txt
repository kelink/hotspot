US copper stretches higher early on durables data
2009-06-24 10:45:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures extended a recovery Wednesday morning from the prior session's dip to three-week lows, on the back of strong durable goods orders and despite slightly disappointing housing data.
Market attention turned to the Federal Reserve's interest rate decision and accompanying policy statement due later in the day.

* Copper for September delivery HGU9 rose 7.25 cents, or 3.3 percent, to $2.2845 a lb by 10:41 a.m. EDT (1441 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* On Tuesday, the benchmark September contract hit a session trough at $2.1235 to mark its lowest level since May 28.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 10,817 lots by 10 a.m.
* Strong durable goods orders a better sign of economic health right now than housing - Sterling Smith, an analyst for Country Hedging Inc. in Inner Grove Heights, Minnesota.
* The Fed's policy statement is due around 2:15 p.m. (1815 GMT) and market awaits its views on how the economic recovery is shaping up and how long the U.S central bank will keep rates near zero to foster growth.
* Recent weakness in copper market tied to speculation China, the world's top consumer, may curtail its near-term purchases.
* China's imports of refined copper hit a record 337,230 tonnes in May, but apparent consumption fell 3.5 percent from the previous month, reflecting increased stockpiles and signaling lower inflows ahead. [ID:nHKG80221]
* The pace of Japanese exports slowed in May as shipments to China deteriorated and U.S. demand remained weak, adding to a gloomy outlook. [ID:nT107592]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> were down 1,225 tonnes to 275,050 tonnes on Wednesday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave LME warehouses -- eased to 17,475 tonnes as of Tuesday from 17,750 tonnes the previous session.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU lost 165 short tons at 60,060 short tons as of Tuesday.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 last traded at $5,025 a tonne, up $220 from Tuesday's close. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
