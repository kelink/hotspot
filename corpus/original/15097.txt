US copper ends down as dollar, quarter-end sales hit
2009-06-30 14:29:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures ended lower on Tuesday, as currency-based selling and quarter-end considerations pulled prices down from an early two-week high and left the market vulnerable to further losses in the days ahead, analysts said.

* Copper for September delivery HGU9 shed 5.40 cents, or 2.3 percent, to settle at $2.2720 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Session range from $2.2370 to $2.3650, a new high for the benchmark contract dating back to June 15.
* Copper succumbs to broader losses in the commodity complex as funds unload at the end of the second quarter - Michael Gross, futures analyst with Optionsellers.com in Tampa, Florida.
* The Reuters-Jefferies CRB index .CRB, a global commodities benchmark, was down about 1.5 percent in afternoon business.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 22,278 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Monday hit 29,867 lots.
* Open interest dropped 1,200 lots to 106,627 open contracts as of June 29.
* Copper up nearly 62 percent in the first half of the year, but expected to meet renewed resistance in the third quarter due to a more stable dollar. [ID:nN30439427]
* Additional downside pressure in copper due to a halted restocking program from China, the world's largest copper consumer. [ID:nPEK124145]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> were down another 1,350 tonnes to 265,950 tonnes on Tuesday.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU stood even at 59,795 short tons as of Monday.
* Chile's industrial production plunged 10.5 percent in May from the same month a year earlier, while the country's copper production fell 2.3 percent in May from a year ago.
* Chile is the world's top producer of the red metal.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 closed down $65 at $4,970 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
