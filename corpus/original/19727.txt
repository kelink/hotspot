US copper futures slip at open, weak data weighs
2009-04-23 10:47:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures sank to a two-week lows below $2.00 a lb before steadying at lower levels Thursday morning, after data showing a further deterioration in the labor and housing sectors of the economy weighed on values.

* Copper for May delivery HGK9 was trading down 0.65 cent at $2.0530 a lb by 10:28 a.m. EDT (1428 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX estimated electronic futures volumes at 14,318 lots by 10 a.m.
* "The (housing) data shows a market that still remains somewhat in the doldrums. We've seen weak purchase data and this shows weakness outside of foreclosures." - Peter Boockvar, equity strategist at Miller Tabak & Co in New York.
* The number of U.S. workers filing new claims for jobless benefits rose to 640,00 in the week to April 18, above economists' forecast for a reading of 635,000. [ID:nN23310355]
* COMEX copper continues to find support near $2.00 a lb amid rising Chinese imports, expectations of higher growth prospects in China, high physical premiums in China, a recent shift in LME spreads into backwardation, and steady inventory declines - RBC Capital Markets.
* London Metal Exchange (LME) copper stocks fell another 9,625 tonnes to 440,475 tonnes on Thursday -- a decline of more than 20 percent since late February. <LME/STX1>
* Canceled warrants stood at 69,175 tonnes on Wednesday -- about 16 percent of total stocks -- from 70,975 tonnes the previous day.
* COMEX copper stocks were unchanged at 47,671 short tons as of Wednesday.
* Imports of refined copper by China, the world's largest consumer accounting for about 30 percent of global demand, rose to a record high of 296,843 tonnes in March, up 137.6 percent from a year ago. [ID:nSP484083]
