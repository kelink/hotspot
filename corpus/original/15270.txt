US copper turns lower with early slide in equities
2009-06-26 11:05:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures erased early gains and dipped into negative territory early Friday, tracking a weaker tone on Wall Street and possibly setting up for further profit-taking losses ahead of the quarter's end next week.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for September delivery HGU9 slipped 1.20 cents to $2.3040 a lb by 10:50 a.m. EDT (1450 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* On Thursday, the benchmark September contract peaked at $2.3465, its highest level since June 15.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 11,513 lots by 10 a.m.
* Copper's losses limited after data showed consumer spending and income up in May and consumer sentiment climbed in June to its highest level since February 2008. [ID:nN26441342]
* Copper further supported by the dollar, which slipped against major currencies as hopes the global financial and economic crisis was moderating eroded the greenback's safe-haven appeal. [USD/]
* A gauge of future U.S. economic growth rose, and its yearly growth rate turned positive, raising hopes that the end of the recession was in sight - The Economic Cycle Research Institute. [ID:nNYS005179]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> shed 1,350 tonnes on Friday to 270,250 tonnes, their lowest level since mid-November.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU were down by 169 short tons at 59,596 short tons as of Thursday.
* Copper inventories in warehouses monitored by the Shanghai Futures Exchange fell 18 percent to 56,088 tonnes from 68,536 tonnes a week ago. [ID:nSP422521]
* Shipments of copper to China, running at record volumes this year because of high local prices, have begun halting at the border after a collapse in Shanghai's premium over world market prices. [ID:nPEK326021]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 traded at $5,040 a tonne, down $90 from Thursday's close. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Walter Bagley)
