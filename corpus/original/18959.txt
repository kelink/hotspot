GLOBAL MARKETS-Stocks rise in U.S. on stress test optimism
2009-05-04 16:49:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. stocks rose on Monday, mirroring gains elsewhere in the world, as investors bet the U.S. government's stress tests won't be that bad for banks.

Asian stocks earlier jumped to a seven-month peak on Monday, fueled by confidence in a global recovery and a jump in Taiwanese stocks. Taiwan's benchmark TAIEX index has gained 12.8 percent in two days as investors see a wide-reaching deal in 2009 that would spark Chinese investment in the island.
"We have China's PMI data, which seems to signal continued recovery for the economy, yet another reason to stay bullish," said Castor Pang, strategist with Sun Hung Kai Financial in Hong Kong.
Longer-dated U.S. Treasury debt prices were flat to slightly higher, with benchmark yields hovering at five-month highs after the Federal Reserve bought a bigger-than-expected $8.5 billion of longer-dated government debt.
