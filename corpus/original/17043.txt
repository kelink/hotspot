FOREX-Dollar stays near 2009 low vs euro and dollar index
2009-06-02 10:45:00.0
(Reuters) - The dollar on Tuesday fell to its lowest level this year against the euro and a basket of currencies, reversing earlier gains, due to a persistent belief that the global economy is on the road to recovery.
The U.S. currency had recovered some of Monday's losses but those gains eroded as the New York session opened. A report on U.S. pending home sales showing the biggest jump in 7-1/2 years in April further helped to renew the recent rally in higher risk currencies. [ID:nN02503338].
Sterling hit a fresh seven-month high versus the greenback, while the Australian dollar rose to a fresh eight-month peak to extend the previous day's gains.
Analysts said the dollar's failure to recover shows that investors are convinced the global economy is over the worst, encouraging them to buy higher-risk currencies and assets.
Pending home sales "has further added to negative sentiment on the dollar," said Omer Esiner, senior currency analyst at Travelex Global Business Payments in Washington. "That said, it's too early to say that we have turned the corner. But it has become apparent that the worst of the credit crisis has passed and that should sap safe-haven demand for the dollar."

Analysts noted the dollar's recent fall has coincided with money shifting out of U.S. Treasuries and into emerging markets equities and other assets.
"It is not so much a flight from the dollar ... but an expressed preference for assets with greater yield-enhancement potential," said Neil Mellor, currency strategist at Bank of New York Mellon in London.
