Chile's peso closes at two-week low, stocks down
2009-07-29 18:00:00.0
(Reuters) - Chile's peso closed at a two-week low on Wednesday due to a stronger U.S. dollar and losses in global bourses, while the leading index ended the day slightly down, traders said.
The blue-chip IPSA index .IPSA slipped 0.13 percent to end at 3,229.30 points, while the all-market IGPA index .IGPA dipped 0.09 percent to close at 15,301.48 points.
"The bourse was in line with New York," said Rodrigo Guzman, an analyst with FIT Research Brokerage.
Wall Street's Dow Jones industrial average
fell 0.29 percent to close on Wednesday at 9,070.72 on investors' worries that China's banks might close the tap on lending, hurting the recovery of the world economy.
Soquimich SQM_pb.SN, one of the world's biggest producers of iodine and lithium, rose 0.42 percent to 19,344 pesos a share, while shares of steelmaker CAP CAP.SN dropped 0.45 percent to 12,339 pesos.
Cencosud CEN.SN, one of Latin America's largest retailers, fell 0.07 percent to 1,489 pesos a share.

The peso has gained 17.55 percent against the dollar so far this year after slumping 22.3 percent in 2008. (Reporting by Froilan Romero; Editing by Diane Craft)
