* The Cosmen family owns 18.6 percent of National Express. Jorge Cosmen, head of Alsa which was sold to the British company in 2005 for 149 million pounds and a 10 percent stake in National Express, is the latter's deputy chairman.
* The Spanish business dynasty started in 1728 by running horse-drawn carriages in the Asturias region, where the family's imprint remains with a street in the main town of Oviedo named Calle Pepe Cosmen after Jorge's father.
* Alsa, founded in 1923, grew significantly after Pepe Cosmen took control. During the 1960s it expanded operations to other European countries such as Switzerland and Portugal and in the 1980s the family ventured into Asia. Alsa is still one of the biggest national bus operators in China.
* Pepe Cosmen is no longer directly involved in the business but his children, including Jorge, are involved in some form with a family business empire that includes hotel holdings, car dealerships and property and alongside the transport interests.
* In December 2005, the Cosmen family bought 14.1 percent of hotel chain AC Hotels for 45 million euros ($54 million).
