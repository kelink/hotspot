PRESS DIGEST - Malaysia - May 7
2009-05-06 20:14:00.0
Track China's Leaders
Wed  EDT
-- A summons will be served on Proton Holdings Bhd PROT.KL to appear in a court in China for an alleged breach of contract case, regardless of the outcome of an arbitration case in Singapore, according to a lawyer representing the plaintiff, Goldstar Heavy Industrial Co Ltd.
-- Retail investors are coming back into the stock market to ride on the current rally, dealers and analysts said.
-- Sindora Bhd SIND.KL, a plantation and investment holding company with subsidiaries involved in car park and shipping businesses, expects to generate revenue of some 200 million ringgit from shipping subsidiaries in 2011, as its fleet size grows to 25 vessels, its Chairman Muhammad Ali Hashim said.
-- Former prime minister Abdullah Ahmad Badawi has been appointed adviser to the development corridors, in recognition of his contributions as the prime mover of these growth centres, Prime Minister Najib Razak made the announcement on Wednesday.
-- Weststar Group, a local outfit controlled by "AP king" Syed Azman Syed Ibrahim, is acquiring UK-based van maker LDV Group Ltd from Russian carmaker GAZ for one pound, sources said.
-- The PricewaterhouseCoopers report on its audit of the Port Klang Free Zone will not be released in the next few days as the Port Klang Authority (PKA) board must agree to the auditor's terms for its release, PKA Chairman Lee Hwa Beng said.
-- Struggling UK van maker LDV, which is currently owned by Russia's GAZ group and had only just last week retrenched most of its 800-plus staff, could be saved by a Malaysian assembler and distributor of its vehicles here, the Weststar Group, according to wire reports.
-- The Al-Bai Bithaman Ajil case that shook the local Islamic banking industry has come to an end as the parties involved are not taking the case to the nation's highest court.
-- Share prices on Bursa Malaysia closed higher on Wednesday on improved sentiment with strong interests seen especially in finance related stocks, dealers said.
