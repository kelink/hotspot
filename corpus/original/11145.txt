ADR Report-Foreign shares dip as investors stay cautious
2009-08-26 16:05:00.0
(Reuters) - Overseas shares traded in the United States slipped on Wednesday, pulled lower by a weak U.S. stock market as investors stayed cautious despite solid data on durable goods and home sales.
Investors were wary of making strong bets after a months-long rally that has seen the S&P 500 gain more than 50 percent from March's 12-year low. Analysts have questioned the staying power of the market recovery, making investors nervous a pullback is due.
