Early US copper his 10-mth peak on economic hopes
2009-08-10 10:51:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures pushed up to new 10-month peak Monday morning, buoyed by a growing sense of optimism about the economy following a string of better-than-expected data from the U.S. and China, the world's top copper consumer.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for September delivery HGU9 rose 2.65 cents to $2.8120 a lb by 10:45 a.m. EDT (1445 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Range from $2.7545 to $2.8465, a new high dating back to early October.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 10,592 lots by 10 a.m.
* Further support from upbeat U.S. employment data on Friday that pulled the unemployment rate down for the first time in 15 months and indicated a positive turn in the world's largest economy. [ID:nN07385157]
* From Japan, machinery orders rose 9.7 percent in June, the first gain in four months and the biggest increase since April 2008. [ID:nLA125957]
* China is due to release a slew of economic data this week, including July's preliminary trade and industrial production data. The country is expected to have lower imports in July, after June's record, but speculative buying is the biggest swing factor. [ID:nHKG361096]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks down 850 tonnes to 291,275 tonnes on Monday. [LME/STX1]
* COMEX copper warehouse stocks eased 93 short tons to 54,112 short tons as of Friday. [CMWSU]
* COMEX copper net short positions shrank 27 percent to 10,654 in the week ended Aug 4. [ID:nN07693350]
* China's demand for copper and stimulus programs in the world's leading economies are likely to help maintain favorable market conditions for the industrial metal - Head of Chile's Codelco, the world's No.1 copper producer. [ID:nN07415766]
* LME Copper for three-months delivery MCU3 last traded at $6,180 a tonne, up $30 from Friday close after hitting new 10-month high at $6,258. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Marguerita Choy)
