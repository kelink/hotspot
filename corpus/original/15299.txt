Momentum buying drives US copper to 2-week highs
2009-06-25 10:32:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures rose to their highest levels in nearly two weeks Thursday morning, as technical momentum kicked in after prices took out the previous session's highs, setting the stage for further gains ahead of the second quarter's end next week.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for September delivery HGU9 firmed 6.00 cents, or 2.6 percent, to $2.3410 a lb by 10:26 a.m. EDT (1426 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Morning range from $2.2450 to $2.3430, its highest level since June 15.
* Technical buy-signal triggered after the overnight break above the Wednesday session peak at $2.3020. Market may drift higher before profit-taking sets in ahead of the weekend and ahead of the end of the second quarter - Larry Young, senior trader at Infinity Futures Inc in Chicago.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 13,481 lots by 10 a.m.
* Initial claims for state unemployment insurance increased by 15,000 to a greater-than-expected seasonally adjusted 627,000 from a revised 612,000 the week before - U.S. Labor Department. [ID:nN25258460]
* Separately, gross domestic product, which measures total output within U.S. borders, dropped at a 5.5 percent annual rate in the first quarter after shrinking 6.3 percent in the fourth quarter of last year and 0.5 percent in the third quarter. [ID:nN25259990]
* Closed arbitrage windows between the Shanghai and London Metal Exchange markets, the summer slowdown and speculation that China's SRB may be looking to sell copper continue to muddy the short-term picture - RBC Capital Markets.
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> dropped 3,450 tonnes to 271,600 tonnes on Thursday to their lowest level since mid-November.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU lost 295 short tons to 59,765 short tons as of Wednesday.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 last traded up $71 at $5,126 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Christian Wiessner)
