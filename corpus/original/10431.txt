ADR Report-Foreign stocks jump on Australia rates, weak USD
2009-10-06 15:11:00.0
(Reuters) - Overseas shares traded in the United States rallied on Tuesday as an interest rate hike from Australia's central bank boosted confidence the global economic recovery is on track.
The U.S. dollar fell across the board, lifting commodities and materials companies, further boosting foreign equities priced in the greenback.
The weaker dollar also pushed U.S. gold futures GCZ9 to an all-time high, with gold mining companies among the top gainers in the session.
Australia's central bank raised its key cash rate by 25 basis points to 3.25 percent, saying it was safe to pull back on stimulus spending. It is the first central bank from the Group of 20 nations to raise rates as the global financial crisis eases. For details, see [ID:nSYD520296].
