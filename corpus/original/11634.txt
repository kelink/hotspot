US copper ends near even after oil, stocks bounce
2009-08-19 17:20:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures recovered from a massive slide by Wednesday's settlement, boosted to nearly unchanged levels when U.S. equity markets and crude oil staged dramatic turnarounds following data showing a surprising decline in U.S. oil inventories, traders said.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for September delivery HGU9 finished with a small 0.10-cent loss at $2.7605 a lb by the close on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Range extended down more than 4 percent to $2.6475, a level dating back to Aug. 3, from a high at $2.7925 a lb.
* COMEX estimated final copper volume at 41,731 lots, compared with Tuesday's tally of 36,196 lots.
* Open interest fell by 2,095 lots to 116,703 open contracts as of August 18.
* Renewed buying in crude oil pulled many commodities, including copper, up from sharply lower levels - traders.
* U.S. stocks turned higher, shaking off a slide in China's equity market, as investors responded favorably to the surprising drop in oil stockpiles. [.N]
* Earlier, a drop in Chinese equity markets led to similar declines in European and U.S. stocks, refueling fears about recovery in global growth and demand for industrial metals.
* Some copper players holding short positions in the $2.50s range have been trying to use any opportunity to sell copper back down to that region. So far, their attempts have been unsuccessful - traders.
* Prior low at $2.6245 a lb for September copper, seen as a key support level, held on Wednesday, suggesting copper is currently in a sideways consolidation mode - traders.
* Sideways pattern should hold in a rough range between $2.65-$2.85 for Sept. copper's near near-term path -traders.
* The dollar fell against the euro and other currencies as U.S. equity markets rebounded, reducing the need for a safe-haven currency. [USD/]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks were drawn down by 750 tonnes to 292,000 tonnes on Wednesday. [LME/STX1]
* COMEX copper warehouse stocks fell by 327 short tons to 52,923 short tons as of Tuesday. [CMWSU]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 fell to $5,981 a tonne by Wednesday's kerb close, down from $6,080 a tonne at Tuesday's close. (Reporting by Carole Vaporean; Editing by Christian Wiessner)
