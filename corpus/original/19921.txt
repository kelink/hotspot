* Copper for May delivery HGK9 fell 1.65 cents to settle at $2.0870 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Stable trade above $2.02 should keep COMEX copper's uptrend intact - Rob Kurzatkowski, futures analyst, OptionsXpress in Chicago.
* COMEX estimated electronic futures volumes at 28,948 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Monday hit 32,539 lots.
* Open interest fell by 1,621 lots to 104,189 contracts open as of April 20.
* COMEX copper opened sharply lower in an extended bout of risk aversion, one day after Bank of America (
) reported a big rise in bad loans, fanning worries about the health of the banking sector and the global economy. [ID:nN20380236]
* Copper's downside limited after inventory data showed London Metal Exchange (LME) copper stocks lost another 5,025 tonnes on Tuesday, bringing total levels to 457,300 tonnes <LME/STX1>.
* Canceled warrants -- stocks earmarked for delivery -- rose to 72,375 tonnes from 68,325 tonnes on Monday.
* Analysts believe the tagged metal, increasingly located in European warehouses, is heading for China.
* COMEX copper stocks held steady at 47,608 short tons as of Monday.
* In currencies, the euro recovered from one-month lows versus the dollar
* China's State Reserves Bureau, the country's commodity buying agency and the driving force behind the latest rally, may release some of its newly bought copper onto the domestic market to push down prices - analysts and traders. [ID:nHKG365218]
* South Korea has issued a tender to buy 7,000 tonnes of refined copper for shipping by June 30, to the port of Gunsan. [ID:nSEO349169]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 was last bid at $4,505 a tonne from $4,590 at the close on Monday. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; editing by Jim Marshall)
