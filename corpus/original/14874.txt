BANGKOK, July 3
- The government has yet to discuss whether Thailand should ask Cambodia to jointly register the ancient Preah Vihear temple on the Thai-Cambodian border as a World Heritage Site, Abhisit said (THE NATION)
- Deputy Finance Minister Pradit Pataraprasit painted a rosy picture by estimating that gross domestic product next year will grow by 4.1 percent -- rather than the widespread current estimate of 2.5 percent -- if the government's stimulus measures can successfully address the economic turbulence (THE NATION)
- Thailand's struggling automotive industry is expected to see a revival by the first or second quarter of next year, (BANGKOK POST)
- Central Trading, a unit of Central Marketing Group, is negotiating a joint venture, possibly with a local partner, in India for distribution of its S'fare men's apparel (THE NATION)
- Two European investors are eyeing aircraft maintenance operations at Don Muang Airport, each planning to commit one billion baht, the Civil Aviation Department said (THE NATION)
- Triumph International's manufacturing unit in Thailand said it had secured interviews for more than 3,900 jobs for the 1,930 workers that the company plans to lay off. It has done so in coordination with the Labour Ministry (THE NATION)
- Thailand's competitiveness ranking shows weakness in labour productivity and human resource development and also in health and the environment, according to Swiss-based Institute for Management Development (BANGKOK POST) ($1=34.12 Baht)
