* HISTORY OF THE SCO AND WHO BELONGS?
-- Besides Russia and China, the SCO groups the ex-Soviet Central Asian republics of Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, Tajikistan and Kyrgyzstan. In addition, Iran, Mongolia, Pakistan and India have observer status. Afghan President Hamid Karzai has been invited as a guest.
-- The member states occupy a territory of around 30,189,000 sq km, or three fifths of the Eurasian continent, and have a population of 1.5 billion, or a quarter of the world's population.
-- The SCO Foundation Declaration was signed at the summit of the heads of the six states held in Shanghai on June 15, 2001. The charter was accepted in 2002 and became effective in Sept. 2003.
-- A Treaty of Long-Term Neighbourliness, Friendship and Cooperation, ratified in Bishkek in Aug. 2007, has substantially solidified the legal foundation of the organisation.
* WHAT DOES THE SCO DO?
The organisations' main aims are to:
-- Strengthen mutual confidence and good-neighbourly relations among the member countries;
-- Promote effective cooperation in politics, trade and economy, science and technology, culture as well as education, energy, transportation, tourism and environmental protection;
-- Make joint efforts to maintain and ensure peace, security and stability in the region.
* WHAT ARE THE AIMS OF THE 2009 SUMMIT?
-- It aims to safeguard peace, security and sustainable development within SCO territory.
-- Combat terrorism, illicit drug trafficking and other types of cross-border crime.
-- Enhancing the international authority and influence of the SCO as an innovative, interstate organisation.
