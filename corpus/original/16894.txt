* Copper for July delivery HGN9 was trading down 4.05 cents, or nearly 1.8 percent, at $2.2570 a lb by 10:50 a.m. EDT (1450 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* On Tuesday, the benchmark July contract climbed to a 7-1/2-month peak at $2.3530.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 8,251 lots by 10 a.m.
* Copper down in sympathy with weaker equities after data showed the U.S. services sector contract further in May, and factory orders fall short of economist expectations.
* The Institute for Supply Management's non-manufacturing index edged up to 44.0 in May, while the Commerce Department said new orders received by U.S. factories rose 0.7 percent in April. [ID:nWEN9983] [ID:nN02175619]
* ISM numbers a bit of a disappointment for the market. Equity markets selling off in reaction, with cautious conditions ahead of Friday's nonfarm payrolls data - Frank Lesh, broker and futures analyst with Future Path Trading in Chicago.
* Chinese restocking has helped drive the price of copper up nearly 60 percent in 2009. China is the world's top copper consumer.
* China's copper consumption is expected to grow 8 percent in 2009 because of robust demand from the cable industry, but supply is still abundant - Wang Zhongkui, deputy general manager of state-owned research firm Antaike. [ID:nSHA263977]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> fell by 2,700 tonnes to 306,525 tonnes on Wednesday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- declined to 37,550 tonnes from 40,175 tonnes the previous session.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU added 205 short tons on Tuesday, to total warehouse levels of 56,030 short tons.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 last traded down $111 at $4,939 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
