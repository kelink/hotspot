US copper ends sharply lower on resurgent dollar
2009-06-15 14:01:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures ended with sharp losses on Monday, as a strong dollar fed a wave of liquidation across the broader commodities complex, helping to drag prices of the metal further away from last week's eight-month high.

* Copper for July delivery HGN9 plunged 8.85 cents, or 3.7 percent, to settle at $2.2850 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Last week, a down dollar, strong import data from China, and upbeat economic data from the U.S. drove the July copper contract to an eight-month peak at $2.4575.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 28,883 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Friday at 33,297 lots.
* Open interest went down by 1,621 lots to 113,5-09 contracts open as of June 12.
* Copper, along with the rest of the commodities, being held hostage to volatility in the currency markets - Ralph Preston, futures analyst with HeritageWestFutures.com in San Diego, California.
* The dollar strengthened after Russia said U.S. currency's role as the world's main reserve currency is unlikely to change in the near future, weighing heavily on broader commodity complex. [ID:nFCA000184] [USD/]
* The Reuters-Jefferies CRB index .CRB, a global commodities benchmark, slumped over 2 percent near a session low at 256.42 in afternoon business in New York.
* Economic data from U.S. showing New York's factory sector contracted at a more severe rate in June added to the industrial metal's bearish price action. [ID:nN15768]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> shed another 3,300 tonnes to 286,975 tonnes on Monday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- slipped to 21,375 tonnes on Friday, from a previous tally of 24,725 tonnes.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU jumped 949 short tons to 59,472 short tons as of Friday.
* COMEX net speculative short position in copper futures grew to 18,769 lots in the week ended June 9, from 18,654 lots the previous week. Open interest dropped to 109,820 contracts open from 111,357 contracts in the week of June 2. [ID:nN1281505]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 shed $224 to settle at $5,006 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Marguerita Choy)
