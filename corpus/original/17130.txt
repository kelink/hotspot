* Copper for July delivery HGN9 surged 12.15 cents, or 5.5 percent, to settle at $2.3190 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Session range from $2.1825 to $2.3285, a new high for the benchmark contract dating back to Oct. 14.
* July copper contract's rally through the top of a triangle consolidation may extend the gains up to a test of key resistance at $2.38 (a lb) -- the major low in February 2007 - CitiFX technicians.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 21,125 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Friday reached 23,820 lots.
* Open interest grew by 2,091 lots to 110,403 contracts open as of May 29.
* China's official purchasing managers' index at 53.1 marked the third consecutive month of expansion of activity in manufacturing. [ID:nPEK17043]
* Manufacturing data, coupled with dollar weakness, upbeat tone in equity markets and top-side technical breakout all combining to drive prices higher - Sterling Smith, vice president with FuturesOne in Chicago.
* Unexpected 0.8 percent uptick in U.S. construction spending in April added to the industrial metal's bullish tone. [ID:nN01456282]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> went down 300 tonnes to 311,975 tonnes on Monday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- fell to 36,700 tonnes from 38,825 tonnes the previous session.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU eased 18 short tons on Friday, bringing total warehouse levels to 55,664 short tons.
* Net speculative short position in COMEX copper futures fell to 17,712 lots in week ended May 26, versus 18,808 lots a week earlier. Open interest rose to 107,631 contracts from 105,791 contracts in the week of May 19 - Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC). [ID:nN29432787]
* Inventory levels of key automotive industrial metals aluminum, copper and steel to remain swollen due to ailing auto demand in the U.S. [ID:nL1544368]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 closed up $245 at $5,075 a tonne. Earlier, it rallied to a 7-month peak at $5,091. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
