US copper recovers, confidence data provide lift
2009-05-26 10:52:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures recovered from overnight losses and headed into positive territory Tuesday morning, after a sharp jump in U.S. consumer confidence helped offset earlier strength in the dollar and waning demand signals from China.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for July delivery HGN9 was trading up 0.25 cent at $2.10 a lb by 10:44 a.m. EDT (1444 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 7,105 lots by 10 a.m.
* "This is very positive, better than expected. I was looking for positive, but this is very solid. This is going to arrest any erosion we've seen in the (stock market) rally.
"This comes on top of the fairly negative home pricing number, but what comes out on top in terms of momentum is consumer confident. It's more relevant and closer to real time. Housing is more backward-looking." - Peter Kenny, managing director at Knight Equity Markets in Jersey City, New Jersey.
* Prices of U.S. single-family homes fell 18.7 percent in March from a year earlier, while prices in the first quarter dropped at a record pace - Standard & Poor's/Case-Shiller Home Price Indices. [ID:nN26481637]
* Demand worries from falling levels of canceled copper warrants and indications of slowing demand from China, the world's leading copper consumer, limiting the red metal's upside potential - analysts.
* China's State Reserves Bureau (SRB) reportedly sold off a small volume of its contracted copper imports over the past month, and might sell up to 50,000 tonnes. [ID:nPEK213195]
Canceled warrants of copper -- material earmarked for delivery -- fell to 47,625 tonnes from 52,875 tonnes on May 21.
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> slipped 6,800 tonnes to 326,575 tonnes on Tuesday.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU went up another 561 short tons to 53,062 short tons as of Friday.
* COMEX net speculative short position in copper futures rose to 18,808 lots in the week ended May 19, from 18,739 lots a week earlier. Open interest was down at 105,791 contracts in the latest week, compared with 108,470 in the week of May 12. [ID:nN22391365]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 was last trading at $4,596 a tonne from $4,610 the previous session. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
