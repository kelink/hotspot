* Futures for the Dow Jones industrial average DJc1, S&P 500 SPc1 and Nasdaq 100 NDc1 rise 0.2-0.3 percent, pointing to a firmer start on Wall Street on Tuesday.
* At 1145 GMT, ICSC/Goldman Sachs will release chain store sales for the week ended Aug. 8. In the previous week, sales fell 0.2 percent.
* Labor Department is scheduled to release, at 1230 GMT, preliminary Q2 Productivity and Unit Labour Costs. Economists in a Reuters survey forecast a rise of 5.3 percent versus a rise of 1.6 percent in Q1. Unit Labor Costs are seen down 2.4 percent compared with a 3.0 percent fall.
* At 1255 GMT, Redbook releases its Retail Sales Index of department and chain store sales for August versus July. In the prior period, sales fell 1.6 percent. * Commerce Department releases at 1400 GMT wholesale inventories for June. Economists in a Reuters survey forecast inventories to fall by 0.9 percent in June versus a decline of 0.8 percent in May.
* At 2100 GMT, ABC News releases its consumer comfort index for the week ended Aug. 9. In the previous week, the index read -49.
* Commodity shares will be in focus after crude oil CLc1 rose 0.5 percent to trade around $71 a barrel, copper MCU3 rose 1.6 percent and aluminium MAL3 jumped more than 2 percent.
