StanChart exits talks over RBS Asia assets-sources
2009-10-04 07:30:00.0
(Reuters) - Standard Chartered's (
) talks to buy assets in China, India and Malaysia from UK rival Royal Bank of Scotland (RBS) (
) have collapsed due to differences over valuation, two people familiar with the matter said.
Reuters reported in August that Asia-focused Standard Chartered was in talks about small acquisitions in China and India, which were likely to cost up to $200 million.
"RBS is in ongoing discussions with bidders for the remaining assets it has decided to sell in Asia and will make further announcements, as appropriate, in due course," RBS said in an emailed statement.

Industry sources have long said the sales could prove complex, with possible corporate customer defections, slowing growth in Asia and unpredictable regulation among the key risks.
