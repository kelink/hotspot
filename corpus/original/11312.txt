CCB's capital adequacy ratio at the end of June was 11.97 percent, down 0.19 percentage points from the end of last year.
Shares of CCB tumbled as much as 6.9 percent in Shanghai, coinciding with speculation, according to traders, that the lender might have to raise fresh capital as a result of pending regulatory changes. Its shares later rebounded somewhat.
China's banking watchdog, concerned that record lending could lead to a spike in bad loans, is proposing to exclude from banks' tier-two capital any subordinated bonds they sell to other banks, sources told Reuters earlier this month. [ID:nSP529534]
The spokesman's comments echoed remarks by a senior CCB official in Hong Kong on Monday, who told reporters that the bank had no plans to raise equity in the near term because its capital was adequate.
