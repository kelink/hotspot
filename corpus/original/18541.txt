US copper ends down in muted response to jobs data

* Copper for July delivery HGN9 slipped 1.90 cents to settle at $2.1455 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Trade encapsulated within previous session's trading range.
* Technical resistance at the $2.24 a lb April peak seen capping an early-morning advance - Frank McGhee, head precious metals trader with Integrated Brokerage Services LLC in Chicago.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 8,251 lots by 10 a.m. EDT (1400 GMT). Final volume on Thursday hit 17,736 lots.
* Open interest rose by 1,679 lots to 106,098 contracts open as of May 7.
* Government data showed the U.S. economy shed 539,000 jobs in April, below the 590,000 expected by economists and the 663,000 jobs lost in March. The jobless rate rose to 8.9 percent. [ID:nN08517208]
* Stream of positive data from the housing, manufacturing and labor sectors of the economy indicate a potential bottom in the downturn and fuel inflationary expectations - Zachary Oxman, managing director with TrendMax Futures in Encinitas, California.
* Imports of refined copper to China, the world's top consumer of the metal, rose to a record high in March, as the inventories on the LME dropped by some 30,000 tonnes.
* London Metal Exchange copper warehouse stocks shed another 4,900 tonnes to 389,000 tonnes -- lowest level since mid-January.
* Canceled warrants -- material tagged for delivery -- were slightly lower at 72,925 tonnes from Thursday's 74,875 tonnes. <MCU/STX4>
* Copper inventories in warehouses monitored by the Shanghai Futures Exchange rose 45 percent to 27,690 tonnes from 19,064 tonnes a week earlier.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU were unchanged at 48,056 short tons as of Thursday.
* Zambia, Africa's top copper producer, selected Chinese firm NFC Africa as the new investors to run the closed Luanshya Copper Mines (LCM), which is due to restart production at the end of May. [ID:nL81044519]
* Mine worker strike at Xstrata's XTA.L Lomas Bayas copper deposit in northern Chile now in its third day. [ID:nN07425585]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 ended down $35 at $4,680 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
