* Copper for July delivery HGN9 was down 0.80 cent at $2.3110 a lb by 10:46 a.m. EDT (1446 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Session range from $2.28 to $2.3530, which marked a new high for the benchmark contract dating back to Oct. 14.
* Monday breakout above multi-month congestion level at $2.24 (a lb) to project a trending move toward $2.40 - Ralph Preston, futures analyst with HeritageWestFutures.com in San Diego, California.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 9,214 lots by 10 a.m.
* Pending Home Sales Index, based on contracts signed in April, rose 6.7 percent in April to 90.3 from 84.6 in March -- the biggest monthly increase since October 2001 - The National Association of Realtors. [ID:nN02503338]
* COMEX copper prices have rallied more than 60 percent in 2009, driven largely by a major restocking process in China, the world's top copper consumer.
* Zambia's state-run power utility is rationing electricity to its vital copper mines because a water weed, introduced from Brazil in the 1990s as a flower, has blocked flows into the biggest hydro power dam. [ID:nL2527519]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> shed another 2,750 tonnes on Tuesday, bringing total warehouse levels to 308,225 tonnes.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- increased to 40,175 tonnes from 36,700 tonnes the previous session.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU rose 161 short tons to 55,825 short tons as of Monday.
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 was last trading at $5,070 a tonne from $5,075 a tonne at the close on Monday. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; editing by Jim Marshall)
