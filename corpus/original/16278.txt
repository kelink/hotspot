Record China imports spur US copper to 8-mth high
2009-06-11 13:56:00.0
(Reuters) - The price of copper rallied to a new eight-month high above $2.45 a lb in New York futures trade on Thursday, as record-high import levels into China, the world's top copper consumer, fed a dollar-induced/technical price breakout this week.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for July delivery HGN9 jumped 7.80 cents, or 3.3 percent, to settle at $2.4450 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Session range from $2.3475 to $2.4575, the highest level for a second-month position contract since October 14.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 19,691 lots by 12 p.m. EDT (1600 GMT). Final futures volume on Wednesday hit 32,077 lots.
* Open interest climbed 3,526 lots to 113,346 contracts open as of June 10.
* China's May imports of unwrought copper and semi-finished copper products hit a fresh high for the fourth straight month on continued arbitrage trade. [ID:nHKG186125]
* Rising Chinese imports piquing short-term speculative interest. Price rally seen as part technical, part speculative - Catherine Virga, senior base metals analyst with CPM Group in New York.
* The dollar slipped as improved U.S. unemployment and retail sales data boosted hopes of an economic recovery and reduced the American currency's safe-haven demand. [USD/]
* Global copper inventories are still low and although metal prices are rising, there is not yet enough demand to increase production - Richard Adkerson, chief executive of Freeport-McMoRan Copper & Gold (
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> fell by 1,100 tonnes to 293,175 tonnes on Thursday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- fell to 27,625 tonnes on Wednesday.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU climbed 971 short tons to 58,484 short tons as of Tuesday.
* U.S. copper imports dropped 32.6 percent in April from the prior month, reflecting lingering demand weakness in construction and manufacturing industries still mired in recession, despite some green shoots of economic improvement. [ID:nN11429715]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 shot up $200 by the close to $5,380 a tonne, just shy of its session peak at $5,388 -- its highest level since mid-October. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by Marguerita Choy)
