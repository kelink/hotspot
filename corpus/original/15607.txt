China demand worries drive US copper to 3-week low
2009-06-22 14:35:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures settled at a three-week low below $2.15 a lb on Monday, as a strong dollar and expectations of waning purchases from the world's top consumer China combined to drag prices lower.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for September delivery HGU9 plunged 11.75 cents, or 5.2 percent, to settle at $2.1440 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 33,530 lots by 1 p.m. EDT (1700 GMT). Final volume on Friday at 29,747 lots.
* Open interest down 402 lots at 113,345 contracts open as of June 19.
* Reuters-Jefferies CRB index .CRB, a global commodities benchmark, down more than 2 percent in afternoon business.
* Speculation of curtailed Chinese copper buying in the near-term producing price set-back in the market - Mike Frawley, global head of metals with Newedge Financial Inc. in New York.
* Closed arbitrage opportunities between the Shanghai and London Metal Exchange (LME), rising inventories at the Shanghai Futures Exchange (SHFE) and an 81 percent decline in canceled warrants -- metal set to leave LME warehouses -- from their recent peak in April support end of China restocking phase - Catherine Virga, senior base metals analyst with CPM Group in New York.
* China's imports of refined copper hit a record 337,230 tonnes in May, up from a previous record of 317,947 tonnes in April and 258 percent higher from a year earlier. [ID:nBJI000174]
* Chinese apparent consumption fell 3.5 percent from the previous month, reflecting increased stockpiles and signaling lower inflows ahead. [ID:nHKG80221]
* World copper market in a surplus of 48,000 tonnes in Q1 - International Copper Study Group (ICSG). [ID:nN22512379]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks <LME/STX1> fell by 2,750 tonnes to 277,600 tonnes on Monday.
* Canceled warrants stood at 16,625 tonnes as of Friday.
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU went up 87 short tons to 60,225 short tons as of Friday.
* COMEX copper net speculative short position eased to 18,464 lots in the week ended June 16, from 18,769 lots the previous week. Open interest grew to 115,321 lots from 109,820 lots during the week of June 9. [ID:nN19679427]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 sank $264, or 5.25 percent, to close at $4,761 a tonne. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
