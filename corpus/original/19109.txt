GLOBAL MARKETS-Oil, global stocks gain on improving sentiment
2009-05-01 17:00:00.0
(Reuters) - Oil prices rose on Friday on a more upbeat U.S. economic news while U.S. stocks closed higher as rising optimism the worst of a deep recession may be over offset selling pressure caused by investors locking in strong gains in April.
The U.S. dollar and yen fell as the data lifted risk appetite and pared demand for both currencies as a refuge for safety against the global slowdown. The data knocked down U.S. government bond prices, sending yields to new 5-month highs.
