US copper eases back from 10-mth peak on dlr gains
2009-08-10 13:58:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures ended lower on Monday, as a third-straight day of gains in the U.S. dollar and surrounding weakness in both equity and energy markets helped pull prices of the red metal down from a new 10-month peak.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for September delivery HGU9 fell 1.50 cents to close at $2.7705 a lb on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Range from $2.7545 to $2.8465, a new high dating back to early October.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 17,804 lots by 12 p.m. EDT (1600 GMT). Final volume on Friday surged to 38,886 lots.
* Open interest fell by 525 lots to 121,379 contracts open as of Aug. 7.
* Copper advance held in check by firmer tone in the U.S. dollar and negative reversal in crude oil prices - Michael Gross, futures analyst with Optionsellers.com in Tampa, Florida.
* Copper market awaiting a slew of economic data this week from China, including preliminary trade and industrial production data for July. The country is expected to have lower imports in July, after June's record, but speculative buying is the biggest swing factor. [ID:nHKG361096]
* China's demand for copper and stimulus programs in the world's leading economies are likely to help maintain favorable market conditions for the industrial metal - Head of Chile's Codelco, the world's No.1 copper producer. [ID:nN07415766]
* London Metal Exchange warehouse stocks down 850 tonnes to 291,275 tonnes on Monday. [LME/STX1]
* COMEX copper warehouse stocks eased 93 short tons to 54,112 short tons as of Friday. [CMWSU]
* COMEX copper net short positions shrank 27 percent to 10,654 in the week ended Aug 4. [ID:nN07693350]
* LME Copper for three-months delivery MCU3 ended down $15 at $6,135 a tonne, after hitting new 10-month high at $6,258. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by David Gregorio)
