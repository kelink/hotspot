(Reuters) - Britain's top share index paused for breath in early trade on Monday after hitting a 10-month closing high on Friday, with heavyweight oils, banks and miners leading the market lower.

was down 32.22 points, or 0.7 percent, at 4,699.34, after closing up 0.9 percent, or 41.03 points, at 4,731.56 on Friday following upbeat employment data in the U.S.
