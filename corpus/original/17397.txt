US copper off early, housing data fail to inspire
2009-05-27 11:00:00.0
(Reuters) - U.S. copper futures retreated from a 2-1/2-week high and remained under pressure Wednesday morning after a mixed report from the housing sector spurred the dollar higher, making the metal less attractive for non-U.S. investors.
For detailed report on global copper markets, click on [MET/L]
* Copper for July delivery HGN9 was down 1.65 cents at $2.1245 a lb by 10:52 a.m. EDT (1452 GMT) on the New York Mercantile Exchange's COMEX division.
* Morning range from $2.1070 to $2.1590 which marked the contract's highest level since May 8.
* COMEX estimated futures volume at 7,540 lots by 10 a.m.
* "Housing numbers a bit of a mixed bag. The supply number rose and I think people were hoping that number would drop by a small amount, and that's kind of keeping the copper a little heavy here." - Sterling Smith, vice president with FuturesOne in Chicago.
* On Tuesday, data showed U.S. consumer confidence soar in May to its highest level in eight months. [ID:nN26484791]
* Another large inventory decline in London Metal Exchange (LME) inventories tempered by the fact that canceled warrants trending lower - MF Global analyst, Edward Meir.
* LME warehouse stock levels <LME/STX1> shed another 7,300 tonnes to 319,275 tonnes on Wednesday.
* Canceled warrants -- metal set to leave warehouses -- went down to 43,375 tonnes from 47,625 tonnes the previous session.
* Copper inventories in warehouses monitored by the Shanghai Futures Exchange fell 11 percent to 30,217 tonnes in the latest week, from 33,798 tonnes last week. [ID:nSP241771]
* COMEX copper stocks CMWSU added another 862 short tons on Tuesday, bringing total warehouse levels to 53,924 short tons.
* High levels of Chinese copper scrap imports not a sign of healthy consumption and might present risks if global demand continues to be hit by the financial crisis - Lili Shi, an independent Chinese scrap metal consultant. [ID:nLR116800]
* Rising inventories of copper and slimming order books for fabricators signaling slowing demand from China, the world's top consumer of the metal. [ID:nHKG228088]
* China's State Reserves Bureau (SRB) reportedly sold off a small volume of its contracted copper imports over the past month, and might sell up to 50,000 tonnes. [ID:nPEK213195]
* LME copper for three-months delivery MCU3 last traded at $4,655 a tonne, down a shade from Tuesday's close at $4,660. (Reporting by Chris Kelly; Editing by John Picinich)
