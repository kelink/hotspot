TAIPEI, June 12
-- Taiwan President Ma Ying-jeou said the island's economy cannot depend solely on China and will try to tap into other emerging markets.
-- The mayor of Taiwan's southern city Tainan, a high-level member of the island's main opposition party, said he plans to visit China on July 18 for a sailboat race ceremony.
-- The executive of a daily commodity maker in China said his company would offer 8,000 workers trips to visit Taiwan in November, bringing in at least T$500 million ($15.2 million) in business to local companies.
-- Kurt Campbell, U.S. President Barack Obama's nominee for assistant secretary of state for East Asian and Pacific Affairs, said Washington would continue selling defensive weapons to Taiwan.
-- Public servants working in the education and defence ministries will be required to pay income tax again from next year, potentially adding T$16 billion to the government coffers, the Commercial Times reported.
