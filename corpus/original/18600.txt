HK shares inch up 0.21 pct led by refiners
2009-05-08 01:41:00.0
(Reuters) - Hong Kong stocks recouped early losses in choppy trade on Friday, rising 0.21 percent as refiners gained on expectations of a fuel price hike, although retailers dropped on a pessimistic industry outlook.

The benchmark Hang Seng Index was up 35.74 points at 17,253.63 by midday.
Turnover slipped to HK$44.2 billion ($5.70 billion) from midday Thursday's HK$56.2 billion.
Many investors tucked away recent profits after the Hang Seng Index rose for six straight sessions. On Thursday, it touched levels not seen since October last year.
Trading was volatile in Friday's morning trading session, with stocks clawing back early losses in the last hour of trade.
The index is at its most overbought position since October 2007, according to its relative strength index (RSI), which reached 80 on Friday. A level above 70 represents an overbought market.
But analysts said market sentiment would be buoyed by rising volumes, a sign of investor confidence. Turnover soared to a seven-month high of HK$95.2 billion on Thursday.
"The Hong Kong market is still enjoying fund inflows from overseas markets," said Kenny Tang, associate director with Redford Securities. "The index is likely to stay above 17,500."
